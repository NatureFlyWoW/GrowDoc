## PRIMARY PERSONAS

### 1. ALEXIS — THE ANXIOUS BEGINNER

**Profile:**
```
Age: 28–35
Location: Urban (US, legal state)
Cannabis experience: Smoker 5+ years, grower 0 years
Tech comfort: High (uses apps daily)
Setup: Micro tent (1m²) with LED, first grow
Budget: $300–500 initial setup
Motivation: Personal use + medical interest (sleep)
```

**Mindset:**
"I've smoked for years but never grown. I'm intimidated by all the information. I don't want to waste money or kill my plant. There's 10,000 conflicting guides online. I don't have time to be a horticulturist—I just want to grow decent weed."

**Biggest Fears:**
1. "Am I killing my plant and not realizing it?" (health anxiety)
2. "What if I run out of electricity?" (legal anxiety)
3. "What if I mess up the nutrients?" (perfectionism)

**How They Currently Solve Problems:**
- Google "cannabis yellowing leaves" → YouTube videos (30+ min)
- Reddit r/microgrowery → wait 1–2 hours for response
- Call a friend who grows (if they have one)
- Panic + do nothing (most common)

**What Would Make Them Successful:**
1. **Instant reassurance:** "Your plant is OK" or "Here's the problem"
2. **Clear daily checklist:** "Do these 3 things today"
3. **Beginner language:** No jargon, explain the why
4. **Progress visibility:** See their plant getting bigger/healthier

**GrowDoc Value Proposition for Alexis:**
"Plant Doctor tells me what's wrong instantly. Daily tasks tell me exactly what to do. I feel like someone's got my back."

**Key Features They'll Use:**
1. Plant Doctor diagnosis (reactive, when worried)
2. Daily task list (habit formation)
3. Growth timeline (progress visibility)
4. Journal + photos (track progress)

**Key Features They'll Skip:**
- Environment calculator (too technical at start)
- Advanced training schedules
- Community features

**Engagement Pattern:**
```
Day 1–3: High anxiety phase
  └─ Diagnosis multiple times per day
  └─ Check plant 5+ times daily
  └─ Read every task obsessively
  
Day 4–10: Confidence phase (if plant looks OK)
  └─ Tasks become routine
  └─ Daily check-in (emotional reassurance)
  └─ Photos for progress tracking
  
Week 2+: Habit phase
  └─ Daily tasks feel normal
  └─ Worried moments trigger diagnosis
  └─ Journal entries automated
  
Post-harvest: Reflection phase
  └─ Compare with goals
  └─ Plan next grow
  └─ Refer friends ("This app saved my life")
```

**Monetization:** Freemium with high conversion probability to premium
- Willing to pay for: photo timeline, analytics, training videos
- Target: $3–5/month
- LTV: ~$50 (10-month lifecycle × 5 grows)

**NPS Drivers:**
- Will recommend if: Plant survived & did better than expected
- Will complain if: Plant died due to missed diagnosis

### 2. JORDAN — THE OPTIMIZATION-FOCUSED INTERMEDIATE

**Profile:**
```
Age: 32–45
Location: Suburban, legal state
Cannabis experience: Smoker 10+ years, grower 3–5 years
Tech comfort: Medium-High (uses spreadsheets, some tools)
Setup: Standard room (4m²), established workflow
Budget: $800–1500 incremental
Motivation: Quality optimization, self-improvement, yield
```

**Mindset:**
"I've grown enough to know I'm not a total noob. But I feel like I'm leaving yield/quality on the table. I've read the books, watched the videos, but I want to dial in MY specific setup. I need concrete data to know if my changes actually work."

**Biggest Challenges:**
1. "How do I know my changes are actually helping?" (measurement problem)
2. "Everyone's advice is for a different setup than mine" (personalization problem)
3. "I'm making decisions on gut feel, not data" (evidence problem)

**How They Currently Solve Problems:**
- Grow journal (detailed notes)
- Spreadsheets (tracking EC, pH, harvest weight)
- YouTube (watching detailed technical channels)
- Reddit (r/microgrowery) + ICMag forums (daily browsing)
- Trial and error (change one thing, see what happens)

**What Would Make Them Successful:**
1. **Personalized recommendations:** Based on MY medium, lights, goals
2. **Outcome tracking:** Prove what works for MY setup
3. **Comparative data:** "Other soil+LED growers achieved X"
4. **Training protocols:** Strain-specific, step-by-step
5. **Analytics:** See trends in yield, terpenes, efficiency

**GrowDoc Value Proposition for Jordan:**
"Plant Doctor keeps me on track. But the real win is the outcome data—now I know what actually works for my setup. The task list is a nice backbone to the day."

**Key Features They'll Use:**
1. Plant Doctor (diagnosis + confidence)
2. Daily task list (structure + consistency)
3. Environment calculator (optimization)
4. Enhanced journal (outcome tracking)
5. Analytics dashboard (premium)
6. Strain database (decision-making)
7. Training scheduler (strain-specific)

**Key Features They'll Skip:**
- Beginner-friendly language options
- Photo timeline (they already do this)

**Engagement Pattern:**
```
Week 1: Setup profile creation
  └─ Feeds in all their existing parameters
  └─ Excited to see personalized tasks
  
Week 2–4: Active optimization phase
  └─ Changes one variable (lights, nutrient EC, humidity)
  └─ Tracks meticulously
  └─ Logs outcomes
  
Week 5+: Monitoring phase
  └─ Daily tasks become autopilot
  └─ Focus on specific optimizations
  └─ Journal entries if something interesting happens
  
Post-harvest: Data analysis
  └─ "My yield was 4.2g/plant, avg 82% THC"
  └─ Reviews outcome data from GrowDoc
  └─ Plans next grow: "Next time, try [this] earlier"
```

**Monetization:** High-value customer
- Willing to pay for: Premium analytics, training, community insights, API access
- Target: $10–15/month
- LTV: ~$150 (15 months × $10) or higher

**NPS Drivers:**
- Will recommend if: Data shows measurable improvement vs previous grows
- Will be critical if: Features are buggy or data is inaccurate

### 3. CASEY — THE DATA-DRIVEN ADVANCED GROWER

**Profile:**
```
Age: 35–50
Location: Rural or well-organized suburban
Cannabis experience: Smoker 15+ years, grower 10+ years
Tech comfort: High (comfortable with APIs, spreadsheets)
Setup: Large room (8+m²) or multiple rooms, specialized genetics
Budget: $2000+ on equipment, willing to pay for premium tools
Motivation: Breeding, pheno-hunting, terpene optimization, legacy
```

**Mindset:**
"I've been growing long enough to have strong opinions. I don't need someone to tell me what to do—I need DATA to validate my decisions. I'm experimenting with genetics, exploring terpene profiles, and building a keeper library. I want tools that integrate with my workflow, not replace it."

**Biggest Challenges:**
1. "How do I organize hundreds of phenotypes?" (scale problem)
2. "What's working vs what I think is working?" (confirmation bias)
3. "How do I compare across multiple grows objectively?" (data integration)
4. "No tools are advanced enough for what I'm doing" (tool gap)

**How They Currently Solve Problems:**
- Detailed Excel sheets (phenotype scoring, yield tracking)
- Written journals (comprehensive notes, photos)
- Photography (plant timeline, trichome tracking)
- ICMag forums + private Discord groups
- Consultant relationships (paid advisors for genetics)

**What Would Make Them Successful:**
1. **Batch tracking:** Multiple grows, phenotypes, comparisons
2. **Advanced analytics:** Yield trends, terpene progression, genetics patterns
3. **Breeding protocols:** Franco-validated genetics guidance
4. **IoT integration:** Connect sensors + grow equipment
5. **API access:** Integrate with existing tools
6. **Community of equals:** Other advanced growers for benchmarking

**GrowDoc Value Proposition for Casey:**
"Honestly, Plant Doctor is useful for handling problems fast. But what really appeals is the outcome database—if I can see aggregated data from advanced growers with similar setups, that validates or challenges my approach. Plus, Franco's genetics guidance could be the edge I need."

**Key Features They'll Use:**
1. Plant Doctor (quick problem-solving)
2. Advanced analytics + batch tracking
3. Breeding/pheno-hunting protocols
4. Community insights (similar advanced growers)
5. Training schedule optimization
6. Strain database deep dives

**Key Features They'll Skip:**
- Daily task list (have their own process)
- Beginner guidance
- Hand-holding

**Engagement Pattern:**
```
Initial setup: 1–2 hours
  └─ Detailed profile creation
  └─ Historical data import (future feature)
  └─ Set up integrations
  
Ongoing (bi-weekly):
  └─ Log batch outcomes
  └─ Review genetics analysis
  └─ Check community benchmarks
  └─ Compare with own data
  
Post-grow (detailed):
  └─ Full phenotype scoring
  └─ Genetics evaluation
  └─ Outcome verification
  └─ Next-grow recommendations
```

**Monetization:** Premium+ customer (high LTV)
- Willing to pay for: Premium+/Enterprise ($25–50/month)
- Wants: Direct Franco consultation, advanced analytics, API, white-label
- LTV: ~$500+ (20 months × $25)

**NPS Drivers:**
- Will recommend if: Data insights help them discover new genetics strategy
- Will demand: Customization, API, direct expert access

## DISCOVERY INSIGHTS

### What Works in Grow Tools (from Grow with Jane + Reddit data)

✅ **What users love:**
- Simple photo upload (shows progress)
- Free tier with no friction
- Multi-plant tracking
- Ability to share/show off grows
- Searchable strain database
- History access ("How did I do last time?")

❌ **What users hate:**
- Confusing UI (too many buttons)
- Slow loading
- Account friction (require login immediately)
- Generic tips (not setup-aware)
- No feedback on accuracy ("Am I right?")
- Ads / upsell pressure

### Entry Points (How Users Discover)

**Primary discovery paths:**
1. Google search: "cannabis plant yellow leaves" → Land on Plant Doctor (SEO win)
2. Reddit link: r/microgrowery users share "Try Plant Doctor"
3. Forum post: "Check out this tool for diagnosis"
4. Word-of-mouth: "My friend uses this, it's great"

**Least effective:**
- App store (we're web-first)
- Paid ads (hard to target in cannabis space)
- Blog content (without existing audience)

## USER RESEARCH RECOMMENDATIONS FOR PHASE 1

### Validation Studies Needed

1. **Task List Usability (Week 1)**
   - A/B test: Generic tasks vs setup-specific tasks
   - Measure: Completion rate, user satisfaction
   - Goal: Ensure tasks feel personalized, not overwhelming

2. **Setup Profile Completion (Week 2)**
   - Track: Abandonment rate by field
   - Test: Required vs optional fields
   - Goal: >80% completion rate

3. **Outcome Check-in Timing (Week 3)**
   - Test: 3-day vs 5-day vs 7-day prompts
   - Measure: Response rate, intrusiveness complaint rate
   - Goal: >70% check-in completion without churn

4. **Retention Cohort Analysis (Week 4+)**
   - Segment by persona (if possible)
   - Track: 7-day, 14-day, 30-day retention
   - Goal: Beginner >50% D7R, Intermediate >70% D7R

### Interview Plan (Post-MVP)

- 5–10 early users per persona (when you reach 50+ users)
- Questions focus on:
  - "What made you come back?" (retention drivers)
  - "Where did you get stuck?" (friction points)
  - "What else would make this essential?" (feature gaps)
  - "Would you pay for X?" (monetization validation)

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR PRODUCT TEAM

1. **The beginner is your growth engine.** Alexis has high churn risk but massive upside. Make the first experience crystal clear.

2. **The intermediate is your retention builder.** Jordan will stay if you deliver outcome data. This is where the defensible moat lives.

3. **The advanced grower is your premium customer.** Casey will pay premium prices for advanced features. Don't sacrifice their experience for beginner simplicity.

4. **Task list is the daily habit hook.** It's not fancy, but it's the single most reliable way to get users back daily. Prioritize task quality over breadth.

5. **Outcome verification is your moat builder.** This is where you differ from all competitors. Make sure it's seamless and trustworthy.

6. **Setup context is everything.** Generic advice is worthless. Every feature must account for medium, lighting, experience level. This is your differentiation.
