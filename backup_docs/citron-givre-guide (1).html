2 Environment — Your VPD Is Critically Wrong

Fix humidity before anything else. Everything flows from this.

Critical — VPD at 1.6 kPa is nearly double the safe range

At 24.9°C and 44% RH, your VPD is roughly 1.6 kPa. A recovering topped clone needs 0.8–1.0 kPa. Your plant's stomata are partially closing to prevent excessive water loss, throttling CO₂ uptake and slowing growth. The plant looks okay because it's surviving — but it's not thriving.

Your current 1.6 kPa is in the danger zone for a recovering clone. Target 0.8–1.0 kPa by raising humidity to 68–75%.

3 Lighting — STIXX 50 Strategy & Flowering Reality

One bar is perfect for recovery. Two bars carry veg. Flower needs more.

Light ramp schedule with STIXX 50 dimmer settings. Two bars cover ~70% of the tent in veg but fall short of minimum flowering PPFD. Plan a 200W upgrade before flipping to 12/12.
Warning — Flowering light budget

Two STIXX 50 bars (108W total, ~300 µmol/s) deliver roughly 469 µmol/m²/s average across your 80×80 tent — about 55% of the minimum recommended for productive flowering. Sanlight's own recommendation for an 80×80 tent is the EVO 3-80 at 200W (545 µmol/s, ~837 µmol/m²/s). The two bars will carry veg perfectly, but budget for a flowering light upgrade (~€350–400) before flipping if you want to do justice to this genetics.

4 Watering & Feeding — The Soil Buffer Window

Your pre-fertilized soil sustains the plant for 3–6 weeks. Use only pH'd water until you see depletion signals.

Water 500–700 mL every 3–4 days using the lift test. Ring-water 5–8cm from the stem to encourage root expansion. Never let the soil pull away from pot edges — that kills mycorrhizal networks.

pH is non-negotiable. Cannabis in soil absorbs nutrients between pH 6.0–6.5. Outside this window, nutrients in the soil become locked out regardless of abundance. Buy a pH pen or drops at Viva Sativa and test every watering. Your tomato earth likely sits at pH 6.5–7.0 (Austrian tomato substrates tend slightly alkaline) — pH your water to 6.0–6.2 to pull the root zone into the sweet spot.

Feeding trigger signals (watch for these around week 3–5): lower leaves turning pale green or yellow (nitrogen depletion), slower growth rate, or light-colored new growth. When you see these, begin BioBizz Bio-Grow at 25% strength (1 mL/L). Since you're running living soil with mycorrhizae, organic liquid nutrients are mandatory — synthetic mineral feeds damage the microbial ecosystem your mycorrhizae depend on.

Note — CalMag under LED

LEDs drive faster photosynthesis with less radiant heat, reducing passive calcium uptake through transpiration. BioBizz CalMag at 1 mL/L from week 4–5 onward prevents the mid-veg calcium lockout that silently stunts growth by 10–30%. This is especially important with your living soil mix, where calcium availability depends on microbial activity and pH.

5 Clone Backup — Insurance for an Irreplaceable Cut

Take backup clones once the plant has 6+ viable branches (week 4–6)

Cloning workflow: select lower branches (higher natural auxin), cut at 45° below a node, dip in Root It gel, and place in pre-soaked rockwool under a humidity dome. Expect 70–90% success rate.

! Devil's Advocate Review

Adversarial self-critique applied to all recommendations above

[CRITICAL] VPD calculation assumes LED leaf temperature offset

The VPD analysis uses a −1°C leaf offset typical for LED-grown plants. However, at 44% RH, transpiration is so high that actual leaf temperature may be 2–3°C below air temperature, making real leaf VPD even higher than calculated. This reinforces the urgency of the humidity fix — the situation may be worse than stated.

→ Fix: No change to recommendation (already maximal urgency). Consider buying an IR thermometer (€15) to verify actual leaf temperature.

[CRITICAL] Tomato earth pH not verified

The guide assumes Austrian tomato earth runs pH 6.5–7.0, but some brands add dolomitic lime pushing pH to 7.2+. At pH 7.2, iron and manganese lockout begins — symptoms that look like deficiency but are actually pH-induced unavailability. Without measuring runoff pH, we're guessing.

→ Fix: Test runoff pH at next watering. Water with pH 6.5 water, collect runoff, measure. If runoff pH is above 7.0, water at pH 5.8–6.0 until runoff drops to 6.2–6.5.

[WARNING] Flowering light shortfall is understated

The guide calls two STIXX 50 bars "adequate for veg" — true — but doesn't state plainly enough that flowering under 108W in an 80×80 tent will produce disappointing results. At 469 µmol/m²/s average PPFD, expect loose, airy buds and roughly 40–60g dry yield. This genetics deserves 800+ µmol. Flowering without an upgrade would waste the Citron Givré's potential.

→ Fix: Upgraded to explicit recommendation: do not flip to 12/12 without a 200W+ light. The EVO 3-80 at ~€380 is the correct match for an 80×80 tent. Budget for this as a hard prerequisite, not an optional upgrade.

[WARNING] Living soil + tomato earth nutrient overlap risk

The guide says "no nutrients for 3–6 weeks" but the tomato earth may contain mineral salts (NPK 14-7-14 type) that combine with the living soil's organic fertility to create an overfed root zone. This was flagged in earlier project discussions — the tomato earth should be limited to 30–40% of the mix. If the mix is 50%+ tomato earth, early nutrient burn (burnt leaf tips) is a real risk.

→ Fix: Monitor leaf tips closely for weeks 1–3. If tips brown or curl, flush with 2× pot volume of pH 6.0 water to wash excess mineral salts.

[WARNING] Carbon filter + extraction will crash humidity further

Once the CAN RK125 (310 m³/h) is installed with the carbon filter, it will exchange the tent's air volume ~7× per minute, pulling moisture out aggressively. Without a humidifier, expect RH to drop from 44% to potentially 30–35%. The guide recommends "run on LOW" but doesn't quantify: at minimum speed, the RK125 still moves ~100 m³/h — enough to exchange tent air 2× per minute.

→ Fix: Humidifier purchase is not optional — it's mandatory once extraction is running. Also consider a fan speed controller (Varispeed) if not already purchased, to fine-tune extraction rate vs. humidity loss.

[NOTE] Clone timing is optimistic

The guide says "6+ branches by week 4–6." After a single topping, realistically expect 4 main branches by week 4, reaching 6+ viable clone sites by week 6–8. The plant needs time to develop lateral branching depth, not just length. Rushing clone cuts from thin branches reduces success rate significantly.

→ Suggestion: Be patient. Week 6–8 is more realistic for first clone cuts. Meanwhile, the plant is safe — just keep it healthy and in veg.

Verdict

Core plan is sound with two critical fixes needed immediately: (1) verify soil pH via runoff test and (2) buy a humidifier before installing extraction. The flowering light shortfall should be treated as a hard blocker, not a "nice to have." Everything else — feeding schedule, clone timing, sourcing — is well-calibrated for the setup and skill level. Fix humidity, fix pH, and this Citron Givré will thrive.
