Jack Herer
Sensi Seeds · Amsterdam · 1994
~55% Sativa ~45% Indica

Jack Herer's true lineage is more nuanced than the commonly cited "Haze × Northern Lights #5 × Shiva Skunk." The original mother was bred by Nevil Schoenmakers before his 1991 arrest: (Skunk #1 × Haze C) × (Northern Lights #5 × Haze C). This means it incorporates two separate Haze lines through the Haze C male — a dark, earthy, Chocolate Thai-dominant phenotype. The genetic ratio: approximately 50% Haze, 37.5% NL#5, 12.5% Skunk #1. Named after the cannabis activist and author of The Emperor Wears No Clothes, it won the High Times Cannabis Cup in 1994 and has since earned 9+ major awards.

Why it feels like "everything is alright"

Jack Herer's terpinolene dominance (found in only ~10% of strains) is the key. Low myrcene prevents couch-lock while terpinolene drives clear-headed, creative euphoria. The secondary β-caryophyllene provides CB2-mediated anxiolysis without sedation.

Glookies
Barney's Farm · Amsterdam
~70% Indica Polyhybrid

Gorilla Glue #4 × Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookies — Barney's Farm confirms the Thin Mint phenotype specifically. What makes this strain genetically remarkable is that Chemdawg genetics appear through at least four independent pathways: Chem's Sister (direct GG4 parent), Sour Diesel in both Sour Dubb and Chocolate Diesel (other GG4 parents), and OG Kush (parent of Thin Mint GSC). GG4 itself was an accident — in 2009, breeder Joesy Whales' plants hermied during a move, and pollen crossed his Chocolate Diesel. From ~5–6 recovered seeds, GG4 emerged.

Super Skunk
Sensi Seeds · Amsterdam · 1990
~80% Indica Your Pheno-Hunt Target

Skunk #1 × Afghani hash plant — released 1990. Skunk #1's crossing sequence was confirmed by Sam the Skunkman: (Afghan × Colombian Gold) × Acapulco Gold, where the first cross produced a late-maturing hybrid and the Acapulco Gold shortened flowering. Super Skunk's additional Afghani cross doubled the indica input, shifting Skunk #1's original ~75% sativa genetics to an 80% indica expression. Your experience — relaxed but not couch-locked, euphoric, giggly — aligns with the sativa-leaning phenotype where Colombian and Mexican heritage breaks through.

OG Kush
Unknown / Josh D · LA · ~1996
~55% Sativa / 45% Indica Disputed Origin

OG Kush's origin is genuinely uncertain even to its principal figures. In the early '90s, exceptional flower called "Krippy" circulated in Florida. Around 1995, Matt "Bubba" Berger moved to LA, rooming with Josh Del Rosso ("Josh D"). In 1996, Berger returned to Florida for cuttings — of ~5 small plants, only one survived the trip. Josh D grew this surviving cut in a Silverlake crawl space, brought it to a Bay Area competition where it scored 10/10 in all categories, and the legend was born. The genetics most likely involve Chemdawg-related genetics plus Hindu Kush/Pakistani indica. By 2001, wholesale hit $8,000/lb.

Cannalope Haze
DNA Genetics · Amsterdam/LA
95–100% Sativa Near-Landrace

Original Haze × Mexican landrace sativa (most likely Michoacán). Neither parent contains intentional indica genetics. Original Haze is a first-generation cross of four pure landrace sativas (Colombian, Mexican, Thai, South Indian), and the Mexican parent is undomesticated. This means 100% of Cannalope Haze's genetics trace to landrace lines within 1–2 generations — closer to wild cannabis than any other strain in this collection. DNA Genetics marketed it as revolutionary: a near-pure sativa finishing in 8–10 weeks instead of Haze's 16–24 weeks. The Mexican parent gets credit — Mexican landraces encode faster flowering without indica dilution. Sativa speed from sativa genes.

Why it smells like melon

Chapter VII

The Caryophyllene Thread

All five strains share a significant β-caryophyllene presence — the only terpene functioning as a dietary CB2 cannabinoid receptor agonist, providing anxiolytic effects through a pathway entirely independent of the GABA system.

β-Caryophyllene presence across all five strains
Tier 1 — β-Caryophyllene & Limonene
Caryophyllene activates CB2 receptors — completely separate from GABA. Bahi et al. (2014) confirmed: a CB2 antagonist fully blocked the anti-anxiety effects, proving the mechanism. Zero risk of cross-tolerance or interference with GABA receptor recovery during benzo withdrawal. Limonene's anxiolytic effects also appear GABA-independent — a 2024 Johns Hopkins double-blind trial showed d-limonene dose-dependently reduced THC-induced anxiety in humans.
Tier 2 — Linalool & α-Pinene
Linalool modulates GABA-A receptors through an allosteric site different from the benzodiazepine binding site (Milanos et al., 2017). This distinction matters: it could theoretically provide gentle GABAergic support without competing for the same receptor site that benzos occupy. Pinene preserves acetylcholine, potentially counteracting cognitive fog during withdrawal.
Tier 3 — Myrcene (use cautiously)
Sedative and muscle-relaxing, but documented barbiturate potentiation flags possible interactions with sensitized GABA systems. Myrcene's indirect GABA modulation suggests caution during benzo withdrawal even though the mechanism differs from direct receptor binding. Present in all five strains but subdominant in the ones you prefer for cerebral effects.
Important safety note

This is pharmacological and botanical information, not medical advice. Benzodiazepine withdrawal can be medically dangerous, involving seizures and other serious complications, and should always be managed with professional guidance. No clinical studies have examined cannabis terpene profiles during active benzo withdrawal specifically. The theoretical advantages of caryophyllene-dominant strains are pharmacologically plausible but unconfirmed in human withdrawal contexts.

Chapter VIII

Pheno-Hunting Protocol

Finding the sativa-leaning, anxiolytic, euphoric Super Skunk from feminized seeds. Estimated probability of finding a meaningfully sativa-leaning expression: 15–25%. With 10–15 seeds, expect 2–3 candidates.

Weeks 1–3
Phase 1 — Solo-Cup Screening
Germinate all seeds (minimum 10–15) in 12–18oz cups with drainage holes. Record germination speed, stem thickness, and leaf shape for each numbered plant. Narrower leaf fingers signal sativa expression. Perform the stem rub test on each seedling — rub the stem between fingers and note aroma. Plants showing citrus or sweet notes rather than pungent earth may lean sativa. Cull obvious runts.
Weeks 3–6
Phase 2 — Vegetative Evaluation & Cloning
Transplant survivors to 1–3 gallon containers. Measure internodal spacing (longer = more sativa-leaning), count leaf fingers, observe branching patterns. Heavy lateral branching signals indica; more apical growth signals sativa. Take clones from ALL remaining candidates before flipping to flower. This is the single most critical step — without clones, any keeper phenotype dies at harvest.
Weeks 6–14
Phase 3 — Flowering Evaluation
Under 12/12, record stretch ratio for each plant. Sativa phenos stretch more aggressively. Note days to first pistils — faster finishers (45 days) skew indica; slower (55+ days) express more sativa. Smell daily during weeks 4–7 using specific descriptors: citrus, sweet, cheese, earth, fuel, ammonia, fruit. The citrus/sweet-forward nose is the strongest predictor of the euphoric, non-sedating phenotype. Watch for hermaphrodite traits — a single "nanner" means immediate culling.
Weeks 14–20
Phase 4 — Post-Harvest Selection
Dry slowly at ~15°C / 55–60% RH for 10–14 days. Cure in glass jars for minimum 2–4 weeks. Test each phenotype separately with a detailed journal: onset character (cerebral rush = desired vs. creeping body = indica-dominant), anxiety response, muscle relaxation quality, mental clarity, euphoria level, duration. Rate each specifically for anxiolytic effect. Promote keeper clones to mother plant status under 18+ hours vegetative light.
Ongoing
Mother Plant Maintenance
Keep the keeper under perpetual veg (18–24h, blue-dominant 4000–6500K). Nitrogen-rich nutrients at lower EC than flowering. Regular pruning promotes lateral branching for clone sites. Take new clones every 4–6 weeks with 2 weeks recovery between sessions. Replace the mother annually with a fresh clone of itself to maintain vigor.
Realistic expectations

From 10–15 feminized Super Skunk seeds: expect 8–12 healthy plants reaching harvest, 1–3 showing notably sativa-leaning expression, and 1 likely emerging as a clear keeper matching the relaxed-but-not-locked, euphoric, giggly profile. Total timeline: 4–6 months from seed to confirmed keeper.
