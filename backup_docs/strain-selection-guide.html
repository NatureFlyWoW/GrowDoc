Why These Terpenes?

Your target profile — β-Caryophyllene + Limonene primary, Myrcene/Linalool/α-Pinene secondary — stacks three independent anti-anxiety pathways without triggering sedation.

A 2024 Johns Hopkins study confirmed that vaporized D-Limonene reduced THC-induced anxiety ratings by more than half in a controlled trial. β-Caryophyllene is the only terpene that directly activates CB2 cannabinoid receptors — producing anxiolysis through the endocannabinoid system without psychoactive CB1 effects.

⚠ Critical for functional use: high Myrcene is the primary driver of couch-lock. Prioritize strains where Caryophyllene + Limonene rank above Myrcene.

β-Caryophyllene → CB2 Receptor

Direct CB2 agonist. Anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic. No psychoactive CB1 activation. Acts like an endocannabinoid without the high — reduces anxiety at the receptor level.

Limonene → 5-HT₁ₐ / Dopamine

Serotonin & dopamine modulation. Mood elevation, stress reduction. Counteracts THC anxiety (Johns Hopkins 2024). The "clarity" terpene.

Linalool → GABA / 5-HT

GABAergic anxiolytic — same pathway as benzodiazepines but without dependence. Enhances serotonin activity at SSRI-target receptors.

α-Pinene → Acetylcholine

AChE inhibitor — protects the neurotransmitter responsible for memory & focus. Counteracts THC-induced short-term memory impairment. The "focus" terpene.

⚔ Devil's Advocate — Terpene Data Honesty

Only AK-47 and Super Lemon Haze have truly independent, quantified terpene data from third-party labs. The rest rely on breeder claims or aggregated community reports. Terpene expression varies significantly by phenotype, growing conditions, harvest timing, and cure — the same genetics can produce different ratios from seed to seed. Treat these profiles as probable ranges, not guarantees. Your best insurance is growing multiple seeds and selecting for the aroma profile you want (peppery + citrus = Caryophyllene + Limonene; earthy + musky = Myrcene dominant).
