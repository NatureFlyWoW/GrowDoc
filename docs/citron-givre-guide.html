Warning · Cookie Lineage Hermie Risk

Watch for nanners (banana-shaped pollen sacs emerging from bud sites) from flower week 3 onward. Cookie lineage is notorious for hermaphrodite expression under stress. Minimize all stressors during flower — no temperature swings above 5°C, no light leaks during dark period, no mechanical damage, no sudden environmental changes. Check bud sites with a 60× loupe every 1–2 days from flower week 3. Remove any nanners immediately with tweezers and clean them away from the plant. One missed nanner can seed the entire plant.

Chapter II · Phase 0 · Days 1–14

The Survival Playbook

The first two weeks with your Citron Givré clone — from unboxing to active vegetative growth. Every action is prioritized by urgency. This is not a reference guide — it's a sequence of decisions, in order, with the reasoning behind each one. Follow the timeline, trust the process, protect the genetics.

Priority #1 · Clone is Irreplaceable

This birthday cut cannot be replaced. Every decision in the next 14 days serves one goal: get this plant healthy, growing, and producing enough vegetative mass to take backup clones before anything else happens. Speed is secondary to survival.

Week 1 — Stabilize

Days 1–3 · Gentle Introduction
Light at 20%, water only if dry
Single STIXX 50 at 20% intensity, 55cm+ from canopy. VPD target: 0.4–0.8 kPa (24–25°C, 65–70% RH). If RH is too low, a cut PET bottle dome over the pot buys time while the plant stabilizes. Day 3 · First Light Increase
Raise dimmer to 40% if plant looks healthy
Healthy signs: leaves pointing slightly upward ("praying"), new growth visible at the topped site, stem firm and green. If wilting or drooping: hold at 20% another 2 days. Don't chase a schedule — chase the plant's response. The plant tells you when it's ready.
Note on Clone Timing — Revised
Take backup clones when material is available: typically weeks 4–7
Do not take clones from a freshly transported, recovering clone in the first week. A stressed plant provides stressed cuttings that are unlikely to root successfully. Instead: take 2–4 cuttings when sufficient lower branching is available — typically weeks 4–7 from a topped clone once it has recovered and is actively growing. If abundant lower branching appears earlier, take clones earlier. Lollipop material (week 7) is ideal: you're removing it anyway, and it's already been light-hardened. Mark weeks 5–7 as your hard window. Two rooted clones before flip. No exceptions.
Days 6–7 · Second Bar Arrives
Install 2nd STIXX 50
Mount the second bar parallel to the first, 20–25cm apart. Run both at 40% initially. Two bars at 40% ≈ 43W actual draw — gentle, even coverage across the 80×80 footprint without overwhelming the recovering plant.

The Transplant — Day 7–10

If the clone arrived in a small pot (1–3L), transplant to a 7–11L fabric pot with your prepared living soil mix. This is your single opportunity to apply mycorrhizae directly to the rootball during this grow.

Transplant Protocol

Water the existing pot 1 hour before transplant — a moist rootball holds together during the move. Fill the new pot halfway with mix. Add mycorrhizae inoculant directly on the soil layer — follow product directions (typically 5–10g per transplant for DYNOMYCO; check your product label). Place the rootball directly on the inoculant. Fill around with remaining mix. Water gently until 5–10% runoff. Do NOT pack soil — roots need air pockets. Living soil benefits from minimal runoff to protect the beneficial biology.

Warning · Tomato Earth pH Check

Austrian Tomatenerde (Tomato Earth) often runs pH 6.5–7.5 with mineral salt pre-loading. Test runoff pH within 24 hours of transplant. If above 6.8, water with pH 6.0–6.3 water for the next two weeks. Most "deficiency" symptoms in the first month are pH lockout from high-pH pre-fertilized soil, not actual nutrient absence.

Week 2 — Recover, Train, Ramp

Days 8–10 · Post-Transplant Recovery
Hold light at 40%, monitor for transplant shock
Transplant shock shows as drooping for 1–3 days. This is normal. Don't water again until the pot feels light on the lift test. The roots are exploring the new soil — let them seek moisture rather than drowning them. VPD: 0.6–0.8 kPa.
Day 10 · CalMag Watch Begins
Monitor for early deficiency signs
Under LED, calcium demand increases faster than under natural light — theoretically due to reduced transpiration at equivalent PPFD compared to HPS (no cannabis-specific controlled comparison exists, but the community consensus is strong). Watch for: random brown necrotic spots on lower/middle leaves (Ca deficiency), or interveinal yellowing starting at leaf margins (Mg deficiency). If either appears, start 0.5 ml/L of a standard 3-2-0 CalMag formula (e.g., CaliMagic) immediately. Prevention beats treatment: start CalMag at day 14 regardless of symptoms.
Days 10–12 · The Fun Begins
Begin LST training
The two branches from the topped site should now be 8–15cm long and showing active growth. Gently bend them outward and down toward horizontal using soft ties or plant clips. Only bend green, flexible stems — never lignified (woody) stems. Goal: expose lower nodes to light, creating future bud sites. The 90° rule: bend the main branch to horizontal and all side shoots become equal vertical growth points.
Days 12–14 · Full Veg Intensity
Ramp both bars to 60–80%
By day 14 the plant should be actively growing — visible new leaf sets appearing every 1–2 days. Ramp STIXX to 60–80% (65–86W actual). Lower hanging height to 35–40cm. VPD shifts to 0.8–1.0 kPa (25°C, 55–60% RH). Begin light organic feeding: compost tea or a top-dress of worm castings applied around the pot perimeter.

Devil's Advocate · First 14 Days Risk Ranking

The biggest risk in the first 14 days isn't underfeeding or wrong light height — it's overwatering. A freshly transplanted clone in living soil has enough nutrients for 2–3 weeks. The lift test (heavy pot = wait, light pot = water) prevents 70% of early-stage problems. The second biggest risk is not securing backup clones when material becomes available. Everything else is recoverable. A dead clone is not.

Chapter III · Equipment & Light

What 108W Delivers

Two Sanlight STIXX 50 bars produce approximately 270 µmol/s total PPF at ~2.5 µmol/J efficiency. In the HS80's reflective interior at the correct hanging distance, that translates to roughly 350–420 µmol/m²/s average PPFD across the 80×80cm footprint.

Light pipeline from wall watts to realistic yield. Training and canopy management are critical at this wattage — every wasted photon hurts proportionally more than at 300W+. Filling the ScrOG net is the primary yield lever available to you.
Research Note · DLI & Yield Saturation

Your STIXX 50 setup delivers approximately 45% of the highest tested DLI levels in published cannabis lighting research (Rodriguez-Morrison et al. 2021, Frontiers in Plant Science), where no yield saturation was observed at those maximum levels. More light would likely produce more yield — but 108W is your constraint, and maximizing canopy fill via ScrOG is the correct response.

Research Note · 13h Photoperiod Option

Ahrens et al. (2024, Plants) found a 13h photoperiod produced 38%+ more THC yield versus 12h with no potency loss across all 10 cultivars tested. Consider running 13/11 instead of 12/12 for the flowering phase. The additional hour allows significantly more photosynthesis per day while still reliably triggering and maintaining flowering in photoperiod genetics. Worth testing on your first run with this cut.

Chapter IV · Vegetative Phase · Weeks 3–8

Training — Single-Plant ScrOG

One plant filling 80×80cm requires 6–8 weeks of veg — not 4–5 as in multi-plant setups. The clone has been topped once, giving you at least two main branches as your canopy framework. Goal: 30–50 evenly spaced bud sites across a flat, light-saturated canopy.

Training progression. With one plant, the net takes 6–8 weeks to fill — be patient. Every empty square at flip wastes photons from your 108W. A fully filled net is worth an extra 2–3 weeks of veg time in final yield.

Research Note · Nutrient Targets (Bernstein Lab)

Published optimal cannabis flowering ranges from Saloner & Bernstein (2020–2022): N ~160 mg/L, P 40–80 mg/L, K 60–175 mg/L. These are hydroponic reference targets — in living soil, biological mediation changes delivery dynamics. Use as directional benchmarks. Potassium in the upper range (100–175 mg/L equivalent) during mid-to-late flower supports bud density and terpene development. A langbeinite or kelp meal top-dress at flower weeks 3–4 addresses this without disrupting the soil ecosystem.

Devil's Advocate · CalMag Under LED

CalMag supplementation under LED is effectively universal practitioner consensus. The theoretical mechanism — reduced transpiration at equivalent PPFD compared to HPS leading to lower passive calcium uptake — is plausible but lacks cannabis-specific controlled comparison data. In practice, mid-flower calcium deficiency under LED in organic soil is common enough to treat as a baseline prevention measure. Start 0.5 ml/L at week 3, rise to 1 ml/L during peak veg and early flower. A small bottle prevents what could become 10–30% yield loss from uncorrected deficiency.

Chapter V · Flip & Flowering · Weeks 8–16

The Flip Protocol

The flip is not a single event — it is a managed transition spanning one week. Stacking 100% light intensity, defoliation, and the 12/12 schedule change on the same day creates a stress cascade that is particularly dangerous for Cookie genetics prone to hermaphrodite expression. Separate these actions across several days.

Warning · Never Stack Flip-Day Stressors

Do not ramp to 100% light, defoliate, and switch to 12/12 on the same day. This stress cascade is a leading cause of hermaphrodite expression in Cookie-lineage genetics. The correct three-step sequence:

Week before flip: Ramp light from 85% to 100% over 3–5 days while still on 18/6 (or 20/4). Flip day: Change timer to 12/12 (or 13/11) only — no other changes. Light is already at 100%. 2–3 days after flip: Perform lollipop pruning and up to 15% fan leaf defoliation. One stressor at a time.

Critical · Two Rooted Clones Before Flip

Take 2–4 cuttings during veg weeks 5–7 (lollipop material = free clones). Cut 10–15cm at 45° below a node, scrape stem 2cm, dip in Clonex or honey, place in jiffy pellet with humidity dome. 80%+ RH, 22–25°C, 7–14 days to root. Two healthy rooted clones must exist before flipping. This is a hard rule. No exceptions.

Week 7 · Pre-Flip Ramp (still on 18/6)
Ramp light to 100%, confirm net is 90%+ filled
Ramp STIXX from 85% to 100% over 3–5 days while still on vegetative photoperiod. Confirm the net has at least 90% canopy fill — 30–50 visible bud sites. Tuck any shoots above the net back under. Perform lollipop pruning (remove all growth below the lowest net level). Do not defoliate fan leaves yet.
Flip Day · Schedule Change Only
Change timer to 12/12 or 13/11. Nothing else.
Flip day = timer change only. Light is already at 100% from the ramp. No defoliation, no training adjustments, no feeding changes. Monitor for light leaks — even a brief light leak during the dark period can delay flowering or trigger hermaphrodite expression in sensitive Cookie genetics.
Days 2–3 Post-Flip · First Defoliation
Remove up to 15% of fan leaves
Two to three days after the schedule flip, perform a moderate defoliation — remove a maximum of 15% of fan leaves. Target leaves that are blocking bud sites or creating dense overlapping canopy areas. Leave at least one full fan leaf per main branch — they power the stretch phase that is currently beginning.
Day 21 Post-Flip · Second Defoliation
Maximum 25% fan leaves total removed
Stretch is mostly complete. Remove leaves blocking bud sites and improve airflow through the canopy interior. This is the last major defoliation — flower sites are hardening and need stability from here. Stop all tucking. Transition to full bloom feeding profile. Begin bud rot checks as flowers swell.
Flower Weeks 3–8 · Cookie Hermie & Rot Watch
Daily nanner inspection + inner bud rot check
From flower week 3 onward: inspect bud sites with a 60× loupe at every watering. Nanners (small banana-shaped pollen sacs emerging from within bud tissue) require immediate removal with tweezers. Minimize all stressors. From flower week 5 onward: Cookie genetics produce dense, compact buds that trap humidity — bud rot risk is above average for this lineage. Check inner bud sites, not just the surface. Signs of botrytis: gray fuzzy growth, isolated browning pistils, soft or mushy feel. Remove affected material immediately with sterile scissors and increase airflow.
Chapter VI · Visual Care Reference

Watering, Training & Leaf Health

Illustrated quick-reference for daily and weekly care routines. These techniques apply throughout veg and into early flower. Your hands and eyes are the best sensors in the tent — no instrument replaces consistent direct observation.

The Lift Test — Watering by Weight

The single most reliable watering method for living soil. Forget moisture meters and fixed schedules. Pick up the pot. Overwatering is the leading cause of failure in the first month, and living soil biology suffers from constant saturation. The lift test prevents both overwatering and the crop failure that comes from missing the "too dry" stage.

The lift test: pick up the pot with one hand. Heavy → wait. Noticeably lighter → water until 5–10% runoff (living soil — less than conventional to preserve biology). Feather-light with visible soil gap at edges → water immediately but slowly (dry soil initially repels water). Veg cycle: every 2–3 days. Peak flower: every 1–2 days. Fabric pots dry significantly faster than plastic.

LST Training — The Bend

Low Stress Training works by bending the main stem to horizontal, breaking apical dominance, and allowing every lower branch to receive equal light. Only bend green, flexible stems during active veg growth. Never bend lignified (woody) sections — they'll snap cleanly and are not recoverable. LST is safe to begin once the two topped branches are 8–15cm long and actively growing.

LST before and after with auxin mechanism illustrated. Bending the main stem to horizontal makes all nodes equally "high" in the plant's hormonal map → equal growth distribution → flat canopy → maximum light capture from 108W. The fundamental technique for single-plant ScrOG success.

Fix pH and watering technique first. Approximately 70% of all plant symptoms resolve within one week of correcting just these two variables. Don't chase multiple deficiency diagnoses simultaneously. Fix the foundation, observe for one full week, then reassess. Only when pH and moisture are confirmed correct should you investigate specific nutrient deficiencies.

Chapter VII · Harvest, Drying & Curing

Harvest — Trichomes, Not Calendar

Harvest timing is determined by trichome observation, not the calendar. Check calyxes (the bud pods themselves), not sugar leaves — sugar leaves amber faster and give a false early reading. A 60× jeweler's loupe is sufficient. Start checking from flower week 6.

Trichome harvest window. Check calyxes, not sugar leaves. 60× jeweler's loupe minimum. Start checking at flower week 6. *The 70/30 milky/amber target is practitioner consensus — no controlled study has correlated specific trichome ratios with optimal subjective quality. Devil's Advocate · Trichome Ratios

The 70/30 milky/amber harvest target is practitioner consensus developed through decades of collective community experience. No controlled study has correlated specific trichome ratios with optimal subjective quality or cannabinoid concentration at the point of harvest. Use the ratio as a directional guide, but trust your developing knowledge of this specific cut — Citron Givré may express its optimal character slightly earlier or later than the standard window.

Research Note · Harvest Timing

Some growers harvest before lights-on, based on unverified community tradition about terpene preservation during the dark period. No peer-reviewed study confirms this timing advantage. If harvesting before lights-on is practical for you (cooler tent temperature, lower stress), that is a reasonable preference — but don't expect measurable quality differences from the timing itself versus harvesting during the first hour of lights-on.

Drying Protocol

The tent becomes your drying chamber. Remove the LED bars. Hang whole branches from the crossbar — whole-branch drying is significantly slower and more even than bucking and rack-drying. A Monkey Fan on its lowest setting creates passive air movement; never point it directly at the buds.

Warning · Drying RH Correction

Target 60–65% RH during drying, not 55–60%. At 55% RH, buds dry too fast — typically 7–8 days — and chlorophyll and ammonia compounds don't have time to break down properly. The result is harsher smoke even after a full cure. At 60–65% RH the drying process sustains the ideal 12–14 day slow dry that produces smooth, complex final product. If your space runs dry in winter, a small humidifier in the dark tent solves this easily.

Research Note · Pre-Harvest Flushing

The only controlled study on pre-harvest flushing (Stemeroff 2017) found no statistically significant benefit to taste, burn quality, smoke smoothness, or mineral content from a water-only pre-harvest period. The traditional "3–5 day water-only flush" is a grower tradition without scientific support. For organic living soil, simply continue normal watering in the final week — the soil biology continues to moderate nutrient availability naturally, and disrupting it with heavy flushing may actually harm final product quality.
