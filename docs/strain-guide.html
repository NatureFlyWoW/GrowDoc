Your Target Profile

Based on your stated preferences, the ideal strain produces: euphoric, cerebral, uplifting effects with moderate body relaxation. Terpene preferences center on citrus-forward, sweet, and spicy profiles — which maps to high limonene + β-caryophyllene + moderate linalool. The common thread across your preferred strains is β-Caryophyllene — the only terpene that functions as a CB2 receptor agonist, contributing to body relaxation and anti-inflammatory effects without sedation.

Why it's interesting: Terpinolene-dominant strains are rare (~10% of cannabis) and produce a distinctly uplifting, almost psychedelic clarity that myrcene-dominant strains can't match. Jack Herer's Haze genetics make it stretch 2–3× in flower — challenging in HS80's 78cm usable zone. Needs aggressive training and early flip. Phenos range from indica-leaning (NL5 dominant) to pure sativa (Haze dominant). The Haze phenos are the ones you want — but they flower for 70–80+ days.

Why it's your chosen grow: Super Skunk is a forgiving, high-yielding indica that's been cultivated indoors for 35 years. The Afghani hashplant parent contributes compact structure, dense resin, and fast flowering — ideal for a first indoor grow in an 80×80cm tent. The sativa-leaning phenos (~5% in fem seeds, 30–50% in regulars) express the Skunk #1's Durban Poison heritage: taller, fruitier, more cerebral. That's your treasure hunt.

The β-Caryophyllene Thread

Every strain in your preferred lineup shares β-Caryophyllene as a dominant or major terpene. This isn't coincidental — BCP is the only terpene that acts as a direct CB2 receptor agonist, producing anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic, and muscle-relaxant effects without the sedation associated with myrcene. It's the terpene that gives these strains their "relaxing but not drowsy" quality. When pheno-hunting Super Skunk, BCP-forward stem rub terpenes (spicy, peppery, clove-like) are a positive selection marker.
