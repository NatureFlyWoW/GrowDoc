1. The Four Super Skunk Phenotypes

SeedFinder's database of 26 indoor grow reviews and ICMag breeder reports identify four distinct phenotypes along two axes: plant structure (tall vs. short) and flowering speed (fast vs. slow). Most feminized seeds express Pheno 3 (short/fast indica). The sativa treasure is Pheno 1 or 2.

The four documented Super Skunk phenotypes mapped by structure and flowering speed. Pheno 1 (sativa/fast) is the target — the blue box — but appears in only ~5% of feminized seeds. Pheno 3 (indica/fast) is what you'll mostly get.

2. Identification Markers — What to Look For

Side-by-side comparison at nodes 3–5. The two most reliable identification markers are internode spacing (wide = sativa) and leaf blade width (narrow = sativa). Look for multiple traits converging in the same plant — no single trait is conclusive on its own.

3. Screening Protocol — 15 Seeds in Your HS80 R2

15 numbered 1L square pots in a 4×4 grid (minus one) under a single STIXX 50 dimmed to 25–50%. Each pot gets a numbered label. Record observations in a simple spreadsheet: plant #, leaf width, internode length, stem rub smell, color, vigor rating.

4. The Selection Decision

After 3–4 weeks, compare all plants side-by-side against the 6 trait markers. The strongest sativa signal is when multiple traits converge in the same plant: narrow leaf blades + wide internodes + lighter green color + citrus stem rub. No single trait alone is conclusive — you need at least 3 of 6 markers pointing sativa to have confidence.

Even if no sativa pheno appears: The screening process still selects the 4 most vigorous, best-structured, best-smelling plants from your seed stock. This alone is a significant upgrade over randomly planting 4 seeds and hoping for the best. The pheno-hunt adds ~3–4 weeks to your timeline but gives you the cream of 15 seeds instead of a random draw of 4.

5. Clone Backup Strategy

Before culling any candidates, take cuttings from the best 6–8. This preserves genetics in case a "backup" plant turns out to be the star performer during flower. Two storage methods work in small spaces:

Shelf method (active)
Root clones under a 15–20W CFL or LED at 18/6. Keep in 0.5L pots. Survives indefinitely with weekly watering. Cost: ~€5.
Fridge method (dormant)
Rooted clone in a sealed bag with damp paper towel at 4–6°C. Survives 2–5 months. Zero maintenance. Check monthly for mold.

6. Feminized vs. Regular — The Strategic Pivot

The fundamental trade-off: feminized seeds simplify growing but nearly eliminate sativa expression. If the sativa hunt is your primary goal, regular seeds dramatically improve the odds — at the cost of needing to identify and cull males.
Devil's advocate — strongest recommendation: If finding a sativa-leaning Super Skunk is genuinely your primary objective (not just a nice-to-have), acquiring regular seeds is the single most impactful decision. Running regular seeds through this same protocol would realistically yield 2–4 sativa-leaning females from 15 seeds — enough to fill the tent. With feminized seeds, the process still produces four excellent indica-dominant plants, but the specific sativa treasure hunt has long odds.
