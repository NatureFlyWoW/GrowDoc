Devil's Advocate — Honest Odds

Feminized Super Skunk is heavily stabilized for indica uniformity. Sensi Seeds markets the feminized line as producing "an extremely uniform crop." From 15 feminized seeds, expect 0–1 plants with genuinely sativa-leaning traits, versus 4–7 from regular seeds. The pheno-hunt process is still valuable — it selects the 4 strongest performers regardless — but if finding the sativa pheno is your primary objective, regular seeds would dramatically improve your odds.

Section 02 · Phenotype Map

The Four Super Skunk Phenotypes

SeedFinder's database of 26 indoor grow reviews and ICMag breeder reports identify four distinct phenotypes along two axes: plant structure (tall vs. short) and flowering speed (fast vs. slow). Most feminized seeds express Pheno 3 (short/fast indica). The sativa treasure is Pheno 1 or 2.

Section 04 · Screening Protocol

15 Seeds in Your HS80 R2

Solo cups in a 4×4 grid under a dimmed STIXX 50 — the most efficient use of 80×80cm for a comparative pheno screen at seedling stage.

Solo-Cup Screening Layout — 15 Seeds in HS80 R2
15 numbered 1L square pots in a 4×4 grid (minus one) under a single STIXX 50 dimmed to 25–50%. Each pot gets a numbered label. Record observations in a simple spreadsheet: plant #, leaf width, internode length, stem rub smell, color, vigor rating.
Section 05 · The Decision Matrix

The Selection Decision

After 3–4 weeks, compare all plants side-by-side against the 6 trait markers. The strongest sativa signal is when multiple traits converge in the same plant: narrow leaf blades + wide internodes + lighter green color + citrus stem rub. No single trait alone is conclusive — you need at least 3 of 6 markers pointing sativa to have confidence.

Even If No Sativa Pheno Appears

The screening process still selects the 4 most vigorous, best-structured, best-smelling plants from your seed stock. This alone is a significant upgrade over randomly planting 4 seeds and hoping for the best. The pheno-hunt adds ~3–4 weeks to your timeline but gives you the cream of 15 seeds instead of a random draw of 4.

Section 06 · Genetic Preservation

Clone Backup Strategy

Before culling any candidates, take cuttings from the best 6–8. This preserves genetics in case a "backup" plant turns out to be the star performer during flower. Two storage methods work in small spaces.

Shelf Method — Active
Living Clone Shelf
Root clones under a 15–20W CFL or LED at 18/6. Keep in 0.5L pots. Survives indefinitely with weekly watering. Cost: ~€5. Allows immediate re-entry to the garden if a keeper is lost.
Fridge Method — Dormant
Cold Storage Clone
Rooted clone in a sealed bag with damp paper towel at 4–6°C. Survives 2–5 months. Zero maintenance. Check monthly for mold. Best for long-term insurance without extra light infrastructure.
Section 07 · Strategic Pivot

Feminized vs. Regular — The Trade-off

The fundamental choice that precedes every other decision in this guide. Feminized seeds simplify the grow but nearly eliminate sativa expression.

Feminized vs. Regular Seeds — Sativa Pheno Probability
The fundamental trade-off: feminized seeds simplify growing but nearly eliminate sativa expression. If the sativa hunt is your primary goal, regular seeds dramatically improve the odds — at the cost of needing to identify and cull males.
Devil's Advocate — Strongest Recommendation

If finding a sativa-leaning Super Skunk is genuinely your primary objective (not just a nice-to-have), acquiring regular seeds is the single most impactful decision. Running regular seeds through this same protocol would realistically yield 2–4 sativa-leaning females from 15 seeds — enough to fill the tent. With feminized seeds, the process still produces four excellent indica-dominant plants, but the specific sativa treasure hunt has long odds.
