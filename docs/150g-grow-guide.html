Devil's Advocate — Yield Recalculation from 150W to 108W

The original "Pushing 200g" guide was written for a Caluma Force 150W (400 µmol/s PPF). Your 2× STIXX 50 produce ~270 µmol/s — 68% of the original light. DLI drops from 23–27 to ~15–18 mol/m²/day on 12/12. This proportionally drops the yield ceiling. The new numbers: 100g floor (ScrOG + basic care), 150g target (full optimization), 175g hard ceiling (perfect pheno + stagger harvest). The guide title has been updated accordingly. Future upgrade path: adding a 3rd STIXX 50 (162W total) would push DLI back to ~22 and the ceiling past 200g.

Section 02

ScrOG — Your Highest-Impact Intervention

With only 108W, canopy efficiency is everything. A flat ScrOG canopy absorbs 95%+ of available photons. An untrained canopy wastes 30–40% on gaps and self-shading. That difference accounts for the gap between 45g and 150g.

Devil's Advocate — Flushing

With organic nutrients, there's nothing harsh to flush — nutrients are microbially processed. A 3–5 day plain-water finish is sufficient. A 10-day flush is unnecessary for organic growing and costs you feeding days during peak bud development.

Section 06

Three Highest-Impact Interventions

If you only do three things beyond basic care, these are the ones. Ranked by yield impact — from the single largest gain down to the most cost-effective improvement with zero spend.

#1 — Worth 40–60g
Flat ScrOG Canopy
90%+ screen fill at flip day. Every unfilled square in the net is a photon wasted. This single intervention is the difference between 45g no-training and 100g+ ScrOG floor. Nothing else comes close.
#2 — Prevents Hidden Loss
CalMag + Mycorrhizae
CalMag at transplant addresses the silent deficiency that blunts yield without obvious symptoms. Mycorrhizae granules (5g on rootball) extend the root system into every corner of your 11L pot. Combined cost ~€20 per run.
#3 — Free
VPD Management
A hygrometer + adjusting fan speed and door gap costs nothing but attention. Keeping VPD in the 0.8–1.2 kPa zone during flower maximises stomatal efficiency and gas exchange — free yield from environmental precision.
