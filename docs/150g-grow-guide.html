1. Light Budget — Recalculated for 2× STIXX 50

Each Sanlight STIXX 50 draws 54W actual and produces approximately 135 µmol/s at ~2.5 µmol/J. Two bars deliver 270 µmol/s total. Inside the reflective HS80 at 25cm height, average PPFD across the 80×80cm footprint is approximately 350–420 µmol/m²/s. During 12/12 flowering, this gives a DLI of ~15–18 mol/m²/day — roughly 45% of the 40 DLI where research shows yield plateaus.

Yield projection scaled to 108W (2× STIXX 50). The jump from "Top + ScrOG" to "Full optimization" comes from VPD control, CalMag, mycorrhizae, aggressive lollipopping, and staggered harvest. All values recalculated from 150W proportionally.

2. ScrOG — Your Highest-Impact Intervention

With only 108W, canopy efficiency is everything. A flat ScrOG canopy absorbs 95%+ of available photons. An untrained canopy wastes 30–40% on gaps and self-shading. That difference accounts for the gap between 45g and 150g.

Top-down view of the fully trained ScrOG canopy at flip day. Each plant fills its 40×40cm quadrant. Every empty square in the net is a wasted photon — fill to 90%+ before switching to 12/12.

3. Training Protocol — Week by Week

Training stages for one Super Skunk plant. Single topping at node 3 + LST produces 4 colas. Fill the net before flipping. Super Skunk's compact indica morphology makes this straightforward — don't overcomplicate with mainlining on a first run.
