01 The Lift Test — Watering by Weight

The single most reliable watering method. Forget moisture meters, forget fixed schedules. Pick up the pot. Your hands are the best sensor you have.

The lift test: pick up the pot with one hand. If heavy → wait. If noticeably lighter → water. If feather-light with visible soil gaps → water immediately but slowly (dry soil repels water initially). Veg: every 2–3 days. Peak flower: every 1–2 days. Fabric pots dry faster than plastic.

02 LST Training — The Bend

Low Stress Training works by bending the main stem to horizontal, breaking apical dominance, and allowing lower branches to receive equal light. Only bend green, flexible stems during active veg growth. Never bend lignified (woody) sections — they'll snap.

LST before and after with realistic plant anatomy. The key mechanism: auxin (growth hormone) concentrates at the highest point. Bending the main stem to horizontal makes ALL nodes equally "high" → equal growth distribution → flat canopy → maximum light capture from your 108W.

03 Leaf Health Quick Reference

Healthy Signs
✓ Leaves angled slightly upward ("praying")
✓ Bright green new growth at tips
✓ Stem firm and green
✓ Visible daily growth during veg
✓ White root tips visible at fabric pot edges
Warning Signs
⚠ Drooping (soft + heavy pot = overwater)
⚠ Yellow lower leaves (N or Mg deficiency)
⚠ Brown spots (Ca def — add CalMag)
⚠ Pale new growth (pH too high — Fe lockout)
⚠ Burnt tips (nutrient excess — reduce 20%)
The 70% Rule — Fix pH + watering first. 70% of all plant symptoms resolve within one week of correcting just these two variables. Don't chase multiple deficiency diagnoses simultaneously. Fix the foundation, then observe.
