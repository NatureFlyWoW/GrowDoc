1. Floor Plan — Top-Down View

Floor plan: tent pushed flush against the right wall, creating a single 40cm service corridor on the left. Power strip, speed controller, and all cable management live in this corridor. Door opens outward into the Diele.
Devil's advocate — tent positioning: Centering the tent creates two 20cm gaps — neither wide enough to reach anything. Right-pushing consolidates all clearance into one usable 40cm corridor where you can kneel, adjust equipment, and route cables. This was a major correction from earlier plans.

2. Side Elevation — Height Stack

Side elevation showing the complete vertical stack. Filter + fan + LED bars occupy the top ~12cm. 25cm clearance to canopy. 86cm usable plant zone. 55cm pot + riser zone. The 72cm gap between tent top and pantry ceiling is your exhaust routing space.

3. Ventilation Routing

Ventilation flow: air enters through passive intake at tent base, rises through canopy, gets pulled through CAN-Lite carbon filter, exhausted by CAN RK125 fan, exits tent top via 125mm ducting. Three critical smell-leak points marked in red.
The fan always exhausts THROUGH the filter, never direct. Air path: tent interior → CAN-Lite carbon filter → CAN RK125 fan → duct out. If you reverse this (fan blowing into filter), the positive pressure pushes unfiltered air out through every tent seam.

4. Electrical Wiring

Electrical wiring diagram. Only the STIXX 50 bars go through the timer (18/6 veg → 12/12 flower). The CAN RK125 fan and Monkey fans run 24/7 directly from the power strip — never on a timer.

5. Floor Protection — Rental Deposit Insurance

Three layers between the tent and the tile floor. This protects against water damage from runoff, condensation, or accidental spills. Total cost: under €10.

Bottom to top: tile → plastic sheeting → towels → tent floor → drip tray. Any water spillage gets caught at multiple layers before reaching the tile. Empty the drip tray 15 minutes after watering.

6. Door Frame Stealth Sealing

The pantry door is your last line of defense against smell, sound, and light leaks into the Diele (hallway). The door frame sealing kit costs ~€15 and is the single most important stealth measure.

Door frame cross-section showing weatherstrip placement and door sweep. This €13 kit is the difference between a stealth grow and detection. Install BEFORE the first seed goes in.
Install the door seal FIRST — before the tent, before the lights, before anything else. In an Austrian Mietwohnung with a flowering cannabis plant, the door seal is literally the difference between success and a very bad conversation with your Vermieter. The CAN-Lite filter handles the air inside the tent. The door seal handles everything that leaks past the tent.

Total build time: ~2.5 hours + 24-hour test run. Do the test run before introducing any plant material. This catches problems (light leaks, fan vibrations, temperature spikes) when they're easy to fix rather than during a critical growth stage.
Devil's advocate — what gets forgotten: The most common build mistakes are: (1) forgetting to tape duct joints (smell leak), (2) putting the fan on the timer (smell during dark period + humidity spike → bud rot), (3) not testing for light leaks during dark period (use a phone camera in a completely dark room — the sensor picks up light the eye misses), and (4) leaving cable clutter in the corridor where it blocks access. Run cables along the wall with cable ties.
