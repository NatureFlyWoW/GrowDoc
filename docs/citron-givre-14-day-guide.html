The First 48 Hours

The clone has just arrived after transport from a trusted contact. It has been topped once and is in a small pot (likely 1–3L). Your first job is not to optimize — it's to not kill it. Resist the urge to repot, feed, or blast it with light. The plant is stressed from transport and needs gentle introduction.

Immediate
Place in tent, STIXX at 20%, water if soil is dry
First 24h
Monitor for wilt, check VPD 0.4–0.8 kPa
48h
If perking up → proceed to Week 1 timeline

Week 1 — Stabilize & Clone

Days 1–3 · Gentle introduction
Light at 20%, water only if dry
Single STIXX 50 at 20% intensity, 55cm+ from canopy. VPD target: 0.4–0.8 kPa (24–25°C, 65–70% RH). If RH is too low, a cut PET bottle dome over the pot buys time. No nutrients, no transplanting, no training. Let the root system settle.
Day 3 · First light increase
Raise dimmer to 40% if plant looks healthy
Healthy = leaves pointing slightly upward, new growth visible at the topped site, stem firm. If wilting or drooping: hold at 20% another 2 days. Don't chase a schedule — chase the plant's response.
Days 4–5 · MOST CRITICAL TASK
Take 2–3 backup clones
If the plant has enough lower branching, take 2–3 cuttings NOW. These are your genetic insurance. Cut 10–15cm at 45° below a node, scrape stem 2cm, dip in Clonex or honey, place in jiffy pellet with a humidity dome (cut PET bottle). Even if they don't root immediately, you've bought time. Don't wait until week 6–8 if material is available now.
Days 6–7 · Second light arrives
Install 2nd STIXX 50
Mount the second bar parallel to the first, 20–25cm apart. Run both at 40% initially. The clone is still settling — don't ramp to full power yet. Two bars at 40% = ~43W actual draw, providing gentle even coverage across the 80×80 footprint.

The Transplant — Day 7–10

If the clone was received in a small pot (1–3L), transplant to a 7–11L fabric pot with your prepared living soil mix. This is your one chance to apply mycorrhizae directly on the rootball.

Transplant Protocol — Water the existing pot 1 hour before transplant (moist rootball holds together). Fill new pot halfway with mix. Add 5g granular mycorrhizae on top of the soil layer. Place rootball directly on the mycorrhizae. Fill around with remaining mix. Water gently until 10% runoff. Do NOT pack soil tightly — roots need air pockets.
Tomato Earth pH Check — If your mix contains Austrian Tomatenerde, test runoff pH within 24 hours of transplant. If above 6.8, water with pH 6.0–6.3 water for the next two weeks to bring the root zone into range. Most "deficiency" symptoms in the first month are actually pH lockout from high-pH pre-fertilized soil.

Week 2 — Recover, Train, Ramp

Days 8–10 · Post-transplant recovery
Hold light at 40%, monitor for transplant shock
Transplant shock shows as drooping for 1–3 days. Normal. Don't water again until the pot feels light (lift test). The roots are exploring the new soil — let them seek moisture rather than drowning them. VPD: 0.6–0.8 kPa.
Day 10 · CalMag watch begins
Monitor for early deficiency signs
Under LED, calcium demand increases faster than in natural light. Watch for: random brown necrotic spots on lower/middle leaves (Ca deficiency), interveinal yellowing starting at leaf margins (Mg deficiency). If either appears, start CalMag at 0.5 ml/L immediately. Prevention is better: start CalMag at day 14 regardless.
Days 10–12 · The fun begins
Begin LST training
The two branches from the topped site should now be 8–15cm long. Gently bend them outward and down to horizontal using soft ties or plant clips. Only bend green, flexible stems — never lignified (woody) ones. Goal: expose lower nodes to light, creating future bud sites. The 90° rule: bend the main branch to horizontal, and side shoots become new vertical growth points.
Days 12–14 · Full veg intensity
Ramp both bars to 60–80%
By day 14, the plant should be actively growing with visible new leaf sets appearing every 1–2 days. Ramp STIXX to 60–80% (65–86W actual). Lower hanging height to 35–40cm. VPD target shifts to 0.8–1.0 kPa (25°C, 55–60% RH). Begin light organic feeding if you haven't already — compost tea or top-dress with worm castings.

Day 14 Checkpoint

By the end of two weeks, your clone should meet these criteria. If any are red, address before moving to the full veg phase.

Growth
2–3 new node pairs visible

Active growth = healthy root system. If still stalled: check pH, watering, and root zone temp (18–22°C optimal).

Genetics
Backup clones taken or scheduled

If not enough material yet, mark week 6–7 as the hard deadline for taking cuts before flip.

Equipment
Both STIXX bars installed, fan + AKF running

Timers installed. Speed controller acquired. Full tent environment verified with 24hr test data.

Next Step — After day 14, transition to the full Citron Givré Complete Guide starting from the "Training — Single-Plant ScrOG" section (week 3+ of veg). The survival phase is over. Now you're growing.
Devil's Advocate — Quick Review — The biggest risk in the first 14 days isn't underfeeding or wrong light height — it's overwatering. A freshly transplanted clone in living soil has enough nutrients for 2–3 weeks. The lift test (pick up the pot: heavy = don't water, light = water) prevents 70% of early-stage problems. The second biggest risk is not taking backup clones when material is available. Everything else is recoverable.
