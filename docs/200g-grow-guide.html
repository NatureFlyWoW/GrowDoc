1. The light is your ceiling — and here's exactly how high it goes

The Caluma Force 150W produces ~400 µmol/s PPF at 2.7 µmol/J. Inside the reflective Q80+ at 25–30cm height, that delivers approximately 540–625 µmol/m²/s average PPFD across the 80×80cm footprint. During a 12-hour flower cycle, this equals a DLI of ~23–27 mol/m²/day — roughly 65% of the 40–45 DLI where peer-reviewed research shows yield plateaus.

What does this mean in grams? Interpolating published yield-vs-PPFD data to your PPFD gives 275–350 g/m². Applied to your 0.64m² canopy: 176–224g dry. Forum-documented results from Rollitup and ICMag with comparable 150W Samsung-diode fixtures in similar tents confirm this range, clustering around 1.0–1.5 g/W with ScrOG.

2. The ScrOG canopy is everything

With only 150W, your single highest-impact intervention is ensuring every square centimeter of the 80×80cm footprint receives equal light. A perfectly flat ScrOG canopy absorbs 95%+ of available photons. An untrained canopy wastes 30–40% on gaps and self-shading. That difference alone accounts for the gap between 100g and 200g.

Top-down: fully trained ScrOG canopy at flip day — ~60 bud sites evenly distributed across 80×80cm. Each plant fills its 40×40cm quadrant. Dashed lines show pot boundaries. Goal: every empty square = wasted photons. Fill the net before flipping to 12/12.

Devil's advocate — training complexity: Mainlining (creating perfect symmetry with 3+ toppings) adds 2–4 weeks of veg time for marginal benefit at 150W. A single topping plus LST produces comparable results in half the time. Super Skunk's indica morphology naturally produces a compact, bushy shape that responds well to a single top — don't overcomplicate it on the first run.

4. VPD targets — your free yield lever

VPD targets by stage with practical instructions for achieving them using only the Varispeed fan controller and door gap. Check min/max memory on digital hygrometer daily at canopy level.
Devil's advocate — VPD realism: Late-flower targets of 40–45% RH may be unachievable during Austrian summer without a small dehumidifier (~€30–50). In winter (the recommended grow season), indoor humidity naturally drops to 30–50% from central heating — making these targets trivial. Schedule lights-on during the hottest part of the day so the dark period coincides with the coolest hours, naturally achieving a 3–5°C temperature DIF.

5. Nutrient schedule — BioBizz optimized with three key supplements

CalMag (non-negotiable under LED)

LEDs drive faster photosynthesis with less radiant heat, reducing passive calcium uptake. BioBizz CalMag at 1 ml/L from week 2 through harvest prevents the mid-flower calcium lockout that silently caps yields by 10–30%.

Mycorrhizal inoculant at transplant

Apply 5g of granular mycorrhizae (DYNOMYCO or Great White) directly on the rootball at transplant. BioBizz organics are ideal partners — synthetic fertilizers suppress mycorrhizal colonization, but organic nutrients don't.

Devil's advocate — mycorrhizae claims: DYNOMYCO's marketing claims "10–45% yield increase." Independent data is more modest — expect 5–15% in well-managed organic soil. Still the highest-ROI supplement at ~€15 per grow, but don't plan your yield around the upper range.

Silica (structural support for heavy colas)

Potassium silicate at 2–3 ml/gallon builds stronger cell walls, supporting heavier colas. Add to water first, before other nutrients, from early veg through week 3–4 of flower.

6. Watering — the most common mistake

The watering cycle. Use the lift test — never a fixed schedule. The "lift test": pick up the pot. If it feels light, water. Veg: every 2–3 days. Peak flower: every 1–2 days. Fabric pots dry faster than plastic. Empty drip trays 15 min after watering.

7. Harvest timing — by trichomes, not the calendar

Start checking trichomes at flower week 6 with a 60× loupe. Check on calyxes (bud pods), not sugar leaves — leaves mature faster and mislead. Super Skunk's real flowering time is 8–9 weeks, not the 45–50 days Sensi Seeds claims.

Trichome stages viewed through a 60× loupe. Always check calyxes, not sugar leaves. The green column is your target window for Super Skunk — complex terpene profile at peak with the characteristic heavy body stone.
Tip — staggered harvest for +10–15% yield: Top buds ripen before lowers. Cut top colas first, strip fan leaves, give lowers 5–7 more days under light. Multiple growers report lower buds "thickening up quite a bit" during this window.

8. Late-flower quality protocol (final 2 weeks)

These interventions are stacked in the last 14 days before harvest for maximum quality.

Intervention What to do Why it works
Temperature drop Days 21–23°C, nights 17–19°C Preserves volatile terpenes, triggers stress-resin response
Humidity drop 35–40% RH Signals plant to produce more trichomes as dehydration defense
Mild drought stress Let pots dry slightly more in final 5–7 days Increases cannabinoid concentration per gram by ~12%
Harvest timing Just before lights-on THC peaks during dark period, degrades partially under light
Devil's advocate — drought stress: Caplan et al. (2019) showed drought stress increases cannabinoid concentration per gram but simultaneously decreases total grams. Net THC per plant stays roughly flat. Use this only as a quality finisher in the final 5–7 days, never as a yield strategy.

9. Drying and curing — where quality is won or lost

Your tent becomes the drying chamber. Remove the LED. Hang whole branches upside-down. Fan on lowest for gentle air exchange. Never point a fan directly at buds.

Phase Conditions Duration Test for readiness
Drying 15–18°C, 55–60% RH, complete darkness 10–14 days Small stems snap cleanly when bent
Cure (week 1) Mason jars, 60–65% RH, dark, 15–21°C Burp 2–3× daily, 10–15 min No ammonia smell when opening
Cure (week 2) Same Burp once daily Boveda 62% pack if below 55% RH
Cure (week 3+) Same Burp weekly Flavor developing, smoke smoothing
Ready Sealed jars, dark, cool Minimum 4 weeks, ideal 6–8 Smooth smoke, complex terpene profile
Tip — dry trimming for quality: Leave sugar leaves on during drying — they slow moisture loss, protect trichomes, and preserve terpenes. Trim to final form only after drying is complete. Super Skunk's dense indica buds benefit from the longer end of the drying range (12–14 days).

10. What NOT to do — debunked techniques

Technique Claim Reality
CO₂ bags +20% yield Your exhaust fan evacuates CO₂ instantly. At 550 PPFD, light is the bottleneck, not carbon. Waste of money.
48–72h darkness pre-harvest +30% THC One unreplicated study. Mold risk in humid tent with no airflow. Skip.
Ice water flush More trichomes A/B test: 26 of 30 strains hermied. Cold shock inhibits P uptake. Don't.
Schwazzing +40% yield Designed for 1000W + CO₂. At 150W, removing that much leaf starves the plant.
13/11 light schedule More yield than 12/12 Promising research but risky with unknown phenos. Stick with 12/12 first grow.

The difference between floor and target is technique and environment. The difference between target and ceiling is experience and phenotype luck. With proper ScrOG, CalMag from week 2, mycorrhizae at transplant, and humidity below 45% in late flower, 200g is a realistic first-grow target.

Three highest-impact interventions (ranked)

1. Flat ScrOG canopy with 90%+ screen fill — worth 40–60g vs untrained plants. This is your single biggest lever at 150W.
2. CalMag + mycorrhizal inoculant — prevents hidden deficiencies that silently cap yields. ~€20 total cost, highest ROI supplement combination.
3. VPD management via canopy-level hygrometer + Varispeed fan adjustment — completely free. Check daily. Adjust fan speed before adjusting anything else.
Best future upgrade: A 200–240W LED pushes DLI from 65% to 85% of optimal and the ceiling past 300g. The tent, pots, ventilation, and technique all carry forward — only the light changes.

Total timeline: ~18–20 weeks seed to smokeable flower including 4-week minimum cure.
