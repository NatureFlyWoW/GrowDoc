# Stealth & OPSEC — Indoor Cultivation Security

## Prime Directive

**A detected grow is a failed grow regardless of yield or quality.** Every recommendation in every other skill must pass through the stealth lens. If a technique maximizes yield but increases detection risk, it's the wrong technique.

---

## Threat Model — Detection Vectors

### Smell (highest risk)

**The timeline:** Cannabis produces detectable odor from ~week 3–4 of veg (stem rub level) escalating to strong room-permeating smell by flower week 3–4, peaking at week 6–8 of flower. Drying/curing is equally dangerous.

**Carbon filter (AKF) is non-negotiable**
- CAN-Lite 300 rated for the HS80 tent. Replace carbon every 12–18 months or when you smell anything outside the tent.
- Run AKF 24/7 during flower AND drying. Not just lights-on.
- Negative pressure: tent walls should suck inward slightly when zipped. This ensures ALL air exits through the filter.
- If tent walls aren't sucking in = air is leaking out unfiltered. Find and seal the leak.

**Odor leak points (specific to Abstellraum setup)**
- Ventilation grate in AR wall → seal with removable magnetic vent cover or foam board + tape
- Door gap at floor → draft excluder / door sweep
- Tent zipper seams → check for pinholes of light (= air leak). Cover with tape or Velcro flap.
- Duct connections → use aluminum tape, not cloth tape. Cloth degrades and leaks.
- During AKF filter change or tent opening → stuff towels under the AR door first.

**Smell discipline**
- Never open tent without checking: is the AR door closed? Is there airflow pulling toward the filter?
- Harvest, trimming, drying = maximum odor events. Do not trim in the living room. Trim inside the tent or AR with filter running.
- Curing jars: burp inside the AR with door closed, never in common areas.
- Hands after handling plants: wash immediately. Terpenes on skin are surprisingly persistent.

### Noise

**Equipment noise sources (ranked)**
1. Inline fan (CAN RK125) — loudest component. Speed controller is CRITICAL.
2. Carbon filter airflow whoosh — muffled by duct, but audible in quiet apartments
3. Clip fan (Monkey Fan) — quiet individually but adds to ambient hum
4. Timer clicks — mechanical timers click audibly. Digital timers are silent.

**Mitigation**
- Speed controller for RK125: run at minimum effective speed. In veg, 30–50% is often enough. Flower with heavy odor: 60–80%.
- **Without a speed controller, the RK125 at full speed is too loud for apartment stealth. Speed controller is a day-1 installation requirement, not optional.**
- Duct silencer (optional, ~€20–30): inline muffler between fan and filter. Reduces whoosh significantly.
- Vibration isolation: hang fan from bungee/rope, not rigid mount. Vibration transfers through rigid connections to walls.
- Timing: run fan at higher speed during daytime ambient noise. Reduce at night when apartment is quiet.
- Cover story for hum: "air purifier" or "dehumidifier running." Both produce similar sounds.

### Heat Signature

**In a small tent (108W LED total):** Heat signature is minimal. 108W ≈ a bright incandescent bulb. Not detectable externally.

**But:**
- Warm air exhaust from tent venting into AR → AR gets warmer than rest of apartment. If maintenance enters AR, warm room + equipment = suspicious.
- Mitigation: exhaust ducting can be routed (if feasible) or the slight warmth is easily explained by "stored electronics" or "router/modem equipment."

### Electrical

**Draw: 108W (lights) + ~30W (fan + clip fan) ≈ 140W continuous**
- During 18/6 veg: ~2.5 kWh/day. Monthly: ~75 kWh. Austrian average household: 300 kWh/month. This is well within normal variation.
- During 12/12 flower: ~1.7 kWh/day. Monthly: ~51 kWh. Even less visible.
- No separate meter, no suspicious draw. This setup is electrically invisible.

**Safety:**
- Don't daisy-chain power strips. Use a single quality Steckdosenleiste (power strip) with surge protection.
- Keep all electrical connections off the floor (water + electricity = fire/shock).
- GFCI/FI-Schutzschalter: Austrian apartments have these in the Sicherungskasten. Ensure the AR circuit is protected. If not, add a portable RCD adapter (~€15).

### Light Leaks

**Two directions of concern:**
1. **Light OUT of tent** — visible under AR door or through gaps = obvious giveaway
   - Check: lights on inside tent, all AR lights off, look for any glow escaping under/around AR door
   - Fix: door sweep, weatherstrip, or towel under door
   
2. **Light INTO tent during dark period** — disrupts flowering (hermie risk)
   - The AR ceiling bulb is the primary risk. If someone turns on the AR light during dark period, even briefly, it's a light leak.
   - Fix: unscrew the AR bulb (store it safely for move-out) or tape the light switch OFF. Use a headlamp (green light) if you need to access AR during dark period.
   - Tent seams/zippers: check for pinholes. Tape any light points.

### Supply Chain / Purchases

**Already established good practices:**
- Cash purchases at local grow shops (La Ganja Loca, Viva Sativa) = no digital trail.
- Receipt management: don't keep receipts at home. Photograph for records, destroy originals.

**Online orders (if needed):**
- Seeds: order to a different name/address if possible, or use a seed bank that ships stealth (hidden in merchandise).
- Equipment: generic items (fabric pots, timers, Epsom salt) can be ordered normally — nothing suspicious individually.
- Avoid ordering everything from one grow shop in a single online order. Spread across vendors if buying online.

### Visitor / Maintenance Management

**Landlord/maintenance visits:**
- Austrian rental law: landlords must give reasonable notice for non-emergency visits.
- Emergency protocol: if same-day notice, the grow must be concealable. In an HS80 tent inside a closed AR behind a door, this is manageable. Ensure:
  - AR door is kept closed and locked if possible
  - No smell outside AR (AKF running)
  - No suspicious sound from outside AR
  - No visible light from under AR door
  - Cover story: "storage room, boxes in there, it's messy" — discourage entry

**Social visitors:**
- AR door always closed when guests are over
- No grow-related items visible in common areas (nutrients, pH meter, etc.)
- Don't discuss the grow with anyone. The single biggest OPSEC failure is human: talking about it.

## Emergency Protocols

### Power Outage
- Carbon filter stops = smell accumulates and vents into apartment
- Action: keep windows openable as backup ventilation. Close AR door. If extended outage, open window in nearest room.
- Light interruption during dark period: brief outage (<1 hour) is OK. Extended = risk of reveg/hermie. Unlikely scenario.

### Water Leak
- Tent floor tray: mandatory. Catches spills and runoff.
- AR floor: tile (per photos) = water-resistant but check for gaps at wall base
- Action: keep towels in AR. Mop immediately. Water damage to downstairs neighbor = emergency + investigation.

### Equipment Failure — Filter
- Smell breakthrough = immediate risk
- Emergency: ONA gel/block outside AR door buys time. Not a replacement for AKF.
- Replace filter immediately. Keep a spare if budget allows.

### Equipment Failure — Fan
- No airflow = heat buildup + humidity spike + CO₂ depletion
- Short-term: open tent, run clip fan at max, keep AR door cracked (only if no visitors expected)
- Replace ASAP. A clip fan alone can't drive air through the carbon filter.

## Stealth Audit Checklist

Run this checklist monthly and before any visitor/maintenance event:

```
SMELL
[ ] Stand outside AR door — any detectable odor?
[ ] Stand at apartment entrance — any odor in hallway?
[ ] Check tent negative pressure — walls sucking in?
[ ] Check all duct connections — aluminum tape secure?
[ ] Ventilation grate sealed?
[ ] Door gap sealed?

NOISE  
[ ] Stand outside AR door — fan audible?
[ ] Stand outside apartment door — any unusual sound?
[ ] Fan speed at minimum effective level?

LIGHT
[ ] During dark period: any light visible under AR door?
[ ] AR ceiling bulb disconnected/removed?
[ ] Tent zippers/seams checked for pinholes?

PHYSICAL
[ ] No grow-related items visible outside AR?
[ ] AR door closed and (ideally) locked?
[ ] No suspicious trash (nutrient bottles, soil bags) in common recycling?

ELECTRICAL
[ ] Single quality power strip, not daisy-chained?
[ ] All connections elevated off floor?
[ ] No overloaded circuits?
```

All stealth-related recommendations are reviewed through `/devils-advocate`.
