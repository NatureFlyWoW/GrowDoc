# Pheno-Hunting Expertise

## Core Principle

Pheno-hunting is structured selection, not vibes. Every keeper decision should be traceable to observable traits scored against a rubric designed BEFORE seeds hit soil. The goal: separate genetic potential from environmental noise.

---

## Phenotype Scoring Framework

### Mandatory Traits (score 1–10, weighted)

| Trait | Observe | When | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vigor** | Internode spacing, growth rate, stem thickness, root dev | Veg wk 3–5 | 15% |
| **Structure** | Branching pattern, node symmetry, training response | Veg wk 4–6 | 15% |
| **Aroma (veg rub)** | Stem rub — complexity, intensity, character | Veg wk 5+ | 10% |
| **Flowering speed** | Days to pistils, stretch ratio, bud set timing | Flower wk 1–3 | 10% |
| **Bud structure** | Calyx-to-leaf ratio, density, foxtailing, bract swell | Flower wk 5–7 | 15% |
| **Resin production** | Trichome density, stalk height, head size, fan leaf coverage | Flower wk 6–8 | 15% |
| **Aroma (flower)** | Complexity, intensity, uniqueness, target profile match | Flower wk 6+ | 15% |
| **Resilience** | Pest/mold resistance, stress recovery, feed tolerance | Throughout | 5% |

### Red Flags (auto-cull unless genetics are irreplaceable)

- Hermaphrodite expression (nanners/bananas) under normal conditions
- Persistent pest magnet when siblings are clean
- Zero terpene expression on stem rub by veg week 6

**Irreplaceable genetics exception:** Hermie risk is managed, not culled — isolate, reduce stress, monitor. Applies to unique cuts like gifted clones.

## Practical Protocol

### Phase 1 — Veg Screening (Wk 3–6)
- Label everything with numbered tags
- Stem rub at week 5+ (lemon, gas, sweet, earthy, pine, chemical, none)
- Measure internode spacing (short = indica-lean, long = sativa-lean within strain)
- Note branching symmetry and lateral vigor
- **Cookie/Chem lineages: do NOT cull at week 3** — slow starters get grace through week 5
- **Aroma scoring delay for Cookie/Chem:** Stem rub may be muted through week 6–7. Early low terpene expression is normal and NOT predictive of flower aroma in these genetics.

### Phase 2 — Early Flower (Wk 1–3 of 12/12)
- Record height at flip + week 3 → stretch ratio
- Time to first pistils
- Bud set pattern (tight clusters vs sparse/airy)

### Phase 3 — Mid/Late Flower (Wk 5–8)
- 60× loupe trichome density comparison across plants at same stage
- Gentle squeeze aroma test on lower buds
- Calyx-to-leaf ratio assessment
- Bud density squeeze test (firm with give = ideal)

### Phase 4 — Post-Harvest
- Dry weight normalized by canopy area
- Cure aroma at 2wk and 4wk (development during cure is a real differentiator)
- Test smoke: onset, peak, duration, character (cerebral/body/balanced)
- Final keeper = best COMBINED score, not best single trait

## Small-Tent Pheno-Hunting (80×80cm)

- **SOG screening:** 4–6 in 3–5L pots, 2–3wk veg, flip early. Screening round, not production.
- **Staggered selection:** Veg screen → clone top 2–3 → flower clones for final selection.
- **Single-plant deep eval:** When running one plant, every data point matters. Document obsessively — n=1 IS your pheno data.

### Noise vs Signal
Consistent traits across multiple nodes/branches = genetic. Asymmetric or position-dependent traits (light-side branching, rootbound compactness) = environmental artifact. For single-plant grows, compare across nodes/branches rather than across plants.

### Single-Plant Mode
With n=1, score against **absolute benchmarks from strain grow reports** rather than relative to siblings. Search ICMag/Grower.ch for the same genetics and use reported internode lengths, stretch ratios, and trichome timelines as your baseline.
