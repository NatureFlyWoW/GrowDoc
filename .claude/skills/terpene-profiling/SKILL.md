# Terpene Profiling Expertise

## Core Principle

Terpenes are the difference between cannabis that gets you high and cannabis that gives you an *experience*. THC percentage is a volume knob; the terpene profile is the equalizer. Quality-first growing means optimizing for terpene expression, not just cannabinoid content.

---

## Major Cannabis Terpenes — Reference

### Primary (>0.5% in high-expression strains)

| Terpene | Aroma | Boiling Point | Key Effects | Found In |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **β-Myrcene** | Earthy, musky, mango, hops | 168°C | Sedating, muscle relaxant, enhances THC absorption | Most indicas, OG Kush, Granddaddy Purple |
| **D-Limonene** | Citrus, lemon, orange peel | 176°C | Mood elevation, anxiolytic, anti-inflammatory | Super Lemon Haze, Jack Herer, Durban Poison |
| **β-Caryophyllene** | Pepper, spice, wood, clove | 130°C | Anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic, **CB2 agonist** (unique among terpenes) | GSC, GG4, OG Kush, Cookies crosses |
| **α-Pinene** | Pine, rosemary, forest | 155°C | Alertness, memory retention, bronchodilator, counteracts THC fog | Jack Herer, Blue Dream, Pineapple Express |
| **Linalool** | Floral, lavender, sweet | 198°C | Calming, anti-anxiety, anticonvulsant | Lavender, Amnesia Haze, LA Confidential |
| **α-Humulene** | Earthy, woody, hoppy | 198°C | Appetite suppressant, anti-inflammatory | GSC, White Widow, Headband |

### Secondary (0.05–0.5%, often defining the "unique character")

| Terpene | Aroma | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Terpinolene** | Piney, floral, herbal, slightly sweet | The "sativa terpene" — rare but dominant in Jack Herer, Durban Poison, Dutch Treat. Uplifting/cerebral. |
| **Ocimene** | Sweet, herbal, woody | Antiviral, antifungal. Found in Clementine, Golden Goat. |
| **Nerolidol** | Woody, fresh bark, floral | Sedating. Enhances skin absorption. Found in Jack Herer (some phenos). |
| **Bisabolol** | Floral, sweet, chamomile | Anti-irritant, wound healing. Found in ACDC, Harle-Tsu. |
| **Geraniol** | Rose, citrus, sweet | Neuroprotectant. Found in Afghani, Headband, Lemon G. |
| **Valencene** | Sweet orange, grapefruit | Insect repellent (literally). Found in Agent Orange, Tangie. |

## The Entourage Effect — What's Real

**Established science:**
- β-Caryophyllene is a functional CB2 receptor agonist — the only terpene with direct cannabinoid receptor activity. This is not speculative.
- Myrcene enhances THC blood-brain barrier permeability (the "mango trick" has a real mechanism, though the magnitude is debated).
- Pinene shows evidence of counteracting THC-induced short-term memory impairment.
- Linalool's anxiolytic effects are well-documented independent of cannabis.

**Probable but less rigorous:**
- Limonene's mood-elevating effects synergize with THC's euphoria.
- Terpene ratios (not just presence/absence) determine subjective effect character.
- The same THC percentage with different terpene profiles produces meaningfully different experiences.

**Marketing hype to be skeptical of:**
- Precise effect predictions from terpene percentages alone
- "Indica = myrcene, sativa = terpinolene" oversimplification
- Any single terpene as a silver bullet for specific conditions

## Terpene-Forward Strain Matching

When a user describes a desired effect, map it to terpene targets:

| Desired Effect | Target Terpenes | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Cerebral, energizing, creative** | Terpinolene, limonene, pinene | High myrcene |
| **Euphoric, social, mood lift** | Limonene, caryophyllene, pinene | High myrcene + linalool combo |
| **Relaxing body, pain relief** | Myrcene, caryophyllene, linalool | High terpinolene |
| **Anti-anxiety without sedation** | Linalool, caryophyllene, low myrcene | Terpinolene (can trigger anxiety in some) |
| **Sleep/sedation** | Myrcene, linalool, humulene | Pinene, terpinolene |
| **Focus, clarity** | Pinene, terpinolene, limonene | High myrcene, linalool |

## Maximizing Terpene Production in Cultivation

### Environmental Levers

- **Late-flower temp drop (last 2 weeks):** 21–23°C days, 17–19°C nights. Terpenes are volatile — lower temps preserve them.
- **UV-B supplementation (flower week 4+):** 2–3 hours mid-photoperiod. Triggers terpene/resin production as UV defense. Most impactful single intervention for terpene density. **Note: requires dedicated UV-B supplement — not present in most LED bars (including Sanlight STIXX 50). Future-cycle upgrade, not current-cycle action.**
- **VPD management:** Moderate drought stress in final 5–7 days concentrates terpenes per gram (but reduces total grams — quality vs quantity trade).
- **Harvest timing:** Just before lights-on. Terpenes degrade under light/heat. Dark period = peak volatile retention.

### Nutritional Levers

- **Sulfur-containing amendments** (Epsom salt, gypsum) support thiol-bound terpene precursors.
- **Potassium** in late flower supports terpene synthase enzyme activity.
- **Organic > synthetic** for terpene expression — multiple forum reports and some research suggest living soil produces more complex terpene profiles.
- **Avoid nitrogen excess in flower** — pushes vegetative growth at the expense of secondary metabolites.

### Genetic Selection (Pheno-Hunt Integration)

- Stem rub test at veg week 5+ is the earliest reliable terpene indicator
- Plants with loud, complex veg aroma almost always produce the best flower terpene profiles
- Zero-aroma plants at week 6 veg rarely develop significant terpene expression in flower

## Terpene Preservation — Drying & Curing

| Phase | Critical Factor | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Harvest** | Cut just before lights-on | Peak terpene retention (dark period) |
| **Drying** | 15–18°C, 55–60% RH, 10–14 days | Slow dry preserves volatiles. Fast dry = harsh, no nose |
| **Dry trim > wet trim** | Leave sugar leaves during drying | Protects trichome heads where terpenes concentrate |
| **Cure (week 1–2)** | Burp jars 2–3×/day | Off-gasses chlorophyll while retaining terpenes |
| **Cure (week 3+)** | Minimal burping, dark, cool | Terpene profile develops and complexifies |
| **Storage** | Mason jars, Boveda 62%, dark, 15–20°C | Light and heat are the enemies of terps |

**Key insight:** The difference between "good weed" and "exceptional weed" is almost always the cure. Terpene profiles at 4-week cure are measurably different from 2-week cure — more complex, smoother, more nuanced.

## Vaporization Temperature Guide

For users who vape, terpene-specific temperatures matter:

**Note:** These are pure-compound boiling points. In flower resin, effective volatilization temps shift ±10–15°C due to the wax/oil matrix.

| Temp Range | What You Get |
|---|---|
| 157–170°C | THC + pinene + caryophyllene. Clear, cerebral, flavor-forward |
| 170–185°C | + myrcene + limonene. Fuller effect, more body |
| 185–200°C | + linalool + humulene. Deep relaxation, full spectrum |
| 200°C+ | Diminishing returns, combustion byproducts approaching |

**Flavor chasers:** Start at 160°C, work up in 5°C increments across a session to experience the full terpene cascade.

## β-Caryophyllene — Special Note

This terpene deserves special attention as the only known dietary cannabinoid — a terpene that directly activates CB2 receptors. It's the bridge between the terpene and cannabinoid systems. Strains high in caryophyllene (GSC, GG4, Cookies crosses, OG lines) tend to have the most "complex" effect profiles, likely because caryophyllene adds a genuine cannabinoid-receptor dimension on top of the terpene-modulated THC effects.

Relevant to the current project: Citron Givré (GSC × Cherry Pie lineage) is expected to be caryophyllene-dominant given both parents' profiles. This is a key trait to verify during the grow.
