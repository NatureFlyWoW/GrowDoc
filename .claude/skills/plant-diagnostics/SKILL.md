# Plant Diagnostics & Troubleshooting

## ⚠️ Context Alert — Active Medium Risk

**Austrian tomato earth (Tomatenerde) can run pH 7.0–7.5 out of the bag.** At this pH, iron and multiple micronutrients lock out before any visible deficiency appears. **Test runoff pH FIRST before diagnosing any deficiency.** This is the #1 most likely issue in the current setup.

## Diagnostic Method

**ALWAYS follow this sequence — don't jump to conclusions:**

### Step 1 — Gather Context (before diagnosing)
- What stage is the plant? (seedling / veg / early flower / mid flower / late flower)
- What medium? (soil / coco / hydro / living soil)
- What nutrients and at what strength?
- Last watering: when, how much, pH of input?
- Runoff pH and EC (if available)?
- Environment: temp, RH, VPD, light distance/intensity?
- Any recent changes (transplant, defoliation, new nutrient, moved light)?
- Duration of symptoms? Sudden onset or gradual?

### Step 2 — Locate the Symptoms
- **Which leaves?** New growth (top) vs old growth (bottom) is the single most important diagnostic clue.
- **Pattern?** Uniform, patchy, interveinal, leaf margins, tips only?
- **Progression direction?** Moving up or down the plant?

### Step 3 — Differential Diagnosis
Use the symptom location + pattern to narrow the cause category, then compare against specific possibilities within that category.

## Quick Diagnostic Map

### Symptoms on OLD leaves (bottom up) → Mobile Nutrient Issue

| Symptom | Most Likely | Confirm By | Common Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Yellow lower leaves, green veins stay** | Magnesium deficiency | Interveinal chlorosis pattern, starts at leaf margins | Low CalMag under LED, pH >6.5 in soil |
| **Uniform yellowing, bottom up** | Nitrogen deficiency | Oldest leaves yellow first, then drop | Underfed in late veg / early flower transition |
| **Purple stems + yellow leaves** | Phosphorus deficiency | Also: dark blueish-green older leaves before yellowing | pH <6.0 in soil, cold root zone (<18°C) |
| **Brown/rust spots on lower leaves** | Calcium deficiency | Random necrotic spots, not following veins | Low CalMag, pH swing, overwatering |
| **Leaf margins brown/crispy** | Potassium deficiency | Starts at leaf edges, progresses inward | Underfed in mid-late flower |

### Symptoms on NEW growth (top down) → Immobile Nutrient or Environment

| Symptom | Most Likely | Confirm By | Common Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New growth pale yellow/white** | Iron deficiency | Interveinal chlorosis on newest leaves specifically | pH >7.0 locking out iron, overwatering |
| **New growth twisted/distorted** | Calcium lockout OR pH crash | Check pH; Ca immobile once deposited | pH swing, excess Mg competing with Ca |
| **Growing tips burnt/brown** | Light burn OR nutrient burn | Light burn = bleached white; nute burn = brown crispy tips | Light too close, or EC too high |
| **Upward leaf curl (taco-ing)** | Heat stress | Leaves curl upward along midrib | Temp >30°C at canopy, light too close |
| **Downward leaf claw** | Nitrogen toxicity | Dark green leaves + clawing tips | Overfed N, especially in flower |

### Pattern-Based Issues

| Pattern | Most Likely | Key Differentiator |
|---|---|---|
| **Burnt leaf tips only** | Nutrient burn (excess) | Tips brown/crispy, rest of leaf OK. Reduce feed 20%. |
| **Interveinal chlorosis (any position)** | Mg def (old) or Fe def (new) | Position on plant tells you which |
| **Random brown spots everywhere** | Calcium def OR CalMag lockout | Check pH. Common under LED without CalMag supplement. |
| **Leaves droop (soft, limp)** | Overwatering | Pot feels heavy. Leaves are soft and droopy, not crispy. |
| **Leaves droop (dry, crispy edges)** | Underwatering | Pot feels light. Leaves curl up and edges crisp. |
| **Spots with concentric rings** | Fungal (leaf septoria / early blight) | Circular spots with bullseye pattern. Humidity issue. |
| **White powder on leaf surface** | Powdery mildew (PM) | Starts as small white patches, spreads. High RH + poor airflow. |

## The pH Lockout Chart

Most "deficiencies" are actually lockout — nutrients are present but unavailable at wrong pH.

**Soil optimal: 6.0–6.8** (sweet spot 6.2–6.5 for cannabis)

| pH | What locks out |
|---|---|
| <5.5 | Ca, Mg, P become unavailable |
| 5.5–6.0 | Ca, Mg reduced uptake |
| 6.0–6.8 | Everything available (optimal) |
| 6.8–7.2 | Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu start locking out |
| >7.2 | Fe severely locked, multiple micros unavailable |

**Austrian tomato earth warning:** Can run pH 7.0–7.5 out of the bag. This alone explains iron deficiency symptoms. Test runoff pH before adding anything else.

## Environmental Stress Diagnosis

| Stress Type | Symptoms | Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Heat stress** | Taco-ing, bleached tops, foxtailing | >30°C at canopy | Raise light, increase ventilation, shift lights-on to night |
| **Light burn** | White/bleached patches on closest leaves | Light too close at high intensity | Raise light 5–10cm, dim if dimmable |
| **Wind burn** | Clawed edges on leaves facing fan | Direct airflow too strong | Redirect fan, reduce speed, oscillate |
| **Cold stress** | Purple stems, slow growth, leaf droop | <15°C root zone or canopy | Raise pot off cold floor, adjust lights-on timing |
| **Root zone too wet** | Drooping, yellow lower leaves, slow growth | Overwatering, poor drainage | Let dry out, improve drainage, fabric pots |
| **VPD too high** | Leaf edges curl up, crispy margins | Low humidity / high temp | Increase RH (humidifier, wet towel, reduce exhaust) |
| **VPD too low** | Slow growth, edema (blisters on leaves) | High humidity / low temp | Increase ventilation, raise temp |

## Pest Identification

| Pest | Signs | Where to Look | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fungus gnats** | Small black flies near soil, larvae in top soil layer | Soil surface, yellow sticky traps | Let soil dry more, BTi (mosquito dunks), neem drench |
| **Spider mites** | Tiny dots on leaf underside, fine webbing | Underside of leaves, use 60× loupe | Neem oil veg only, predatory mites (Phytoseiulus), spinosad |
| **Thrips** | Silver streaks on leaves, tiny elongated bugs | Leaf surface, hiding in leaf folds | Spinosad, sticky traps, neem in veg |
| **Aphids** | Clusters on stems/undersides, sticky honeydew | New growth, stem junctions | Neem, insecticidal soap in veg; ladybugs (not in tent) |
| **Powdery mildew** | White patches on leaf surface | Upper side of fan leaves, low-airflow areas | Improve airflow, potassium bicarbonate spray, remove affected leaves |
| **Bud rot (Botrytis)** | Grey/brown mushy spot inside bud, leaves yellow/die at single bud | Inside densest buds, starts at stem | Remove affected bud + 2cm margin. Increase airflow, lower RH. |

**IPM (Integrated Pest Management) priority:**
1. Prevention: clean tent, yellow sticky traps from day 1, air circulation
2. Cultural: healthy plant = resistant plant. Proper VPD + nutrition is the best IPM
3. Biological: beneficial insects/microbes
4. Organic sprays: neem, spinosad, potassium bicarb — VEG ONLY for sprays
5. NEVER spray anything on buds in flower. Period.
6. **Stop ALL foliar sprays (including neem) at least 7 days before flip to 12/12.** Residual taste/smell carries into early flower.

## Hermaphrodite Detection

**Male flowers (nanners/bananas)**
- Appear as small banana-shaped pollen sacs emerging from bud sites
- Can appear mid-to-late flower, especially under stress (light leaks, temp swings, root stress)
- Pluck immediately with tweezers if found. If widespread = plant is compromised.
- For irreplaceable genetics: pluck, reduce stress, monitor daily. Consider emergency harvest if pollen is released.

**Intersex indicators**
- Mixed pistils and pollen sacs at the same node
- Stress-induced vs genetic: stress hermies appear under bad conditions; genetic hermies appear even under perfect conditions.
- Genetic hermie = cull (unreliable genetics). Stress hermie = fix the stress, plant may recover.

## Troubleshooting Decision Tree

```
Plant looks wrong
├── DROOPING?
│   ├── Pot heavy → Overwatering. Wait.
│   └── Pot light → Underwatering. Water now.
├── YELLOWING?
│   ├── Bottom leaves → Check N, Mg, pH
│   ├── Top leaves → Check Fe, pH >7?
│   └── Everywhere → pH lockout or root issue
├── SPOTS/BURNS?
│   ├── Tips only → Nute burn. Reduce 20%.
│   ├── Random spots → Ca def. Add CalMag, check pH.
│   └── Circular pattern → Fungal. Airflow + treatment.
├── CURLING?
│   ├── Upward (taco) → Heat or VPD too high
│   ├── Downward (claw) → N toxicity or overwater
│   └── Edges only → Wind burn or K def
└── WHITE/POWDERY?
    ├── On leaf surface → PM. Airflow + K-bicarb.
    └── Bleached patches → Light burn. Raise light.
```

## Critical Reminders

- **The most common cause of "deficiency" in organic soil is pH lockout.** Always test pH first.
- **The second most common cause is overwatering.** Roots in standing water can't uptake anything.
- **Under LED, CalMag deficiency is the #1 hidden yield killer.** LEDs drive photosynthesis faster with less radiant heat, increasing Ca/Mg demand while reducing passive Ca uptake.
- **Don't chase multiple deficiencies at once.** Fix pH + watering first. 70% of symptoms resolve within a week.
- **Photos are 10× better than descriptions.** Always ask for photos when diagnosing remotely.
