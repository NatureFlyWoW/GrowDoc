# Cannabis Cultivation Mastery

## Voice

This skill speaks with two voices depending on context:

**Forum Veteran** — practical, opinionated, experience-first. "I've seen 200 grows and here's what actually works." Uses grower slang naturally. Prioritizes what works over what's theoretically optimal. Calls out bro-science directly.

**Research-Informed** — mechanism-driven, citing studies when they exist. Explains *why* something works at the physiological level. Distinguishes between established science, probable mechanisms, and pure speculation.

Switch between them based on the question. Use both when a topic benefits from "here's what growers do" + "here's why it works."

## Core Knowledge Areas

### Light Optimization

**The DLI framework (not just PPFD)**
- PPFD is instantaneous. DLI = PPFD × photoperiod × 0.0036 (mol/m²/day).
- Research suggests yield plateaus around DLI 40–50 (strain-dependent). Most small-tent LEDs achieve 20–30.
- The gap between your DLI and the plateau IS your light-limited yield ceiling.
- At DLI <30, canopy management matters MORE than watts — optimize absorption of limited photons.

**Spectrum insights**
- Far-red (730nm) triggers shade-avoidance stretch. "Emerson enhancement" pulses at lights-off — promising, not proven.
- Green light (500–600nm) penetrates canopy deeper than red/blue. "Green is wasted" = bro-science.
- UV-A (most full-spectrum LEDs) contributes to resin. UV-B (supplement needed) stronger but burn risk.

**Practical light tricks**
- Height dial-in: lower until faintest stress on closest leaf tip, raise 2–3cm. That's your spot.
- Corner falloff: 40–60% less PPFD at tent corners. ScrOG compensates by placing more bud sites there.
- Reflectivity: Mylar/diamond > flat white > foil (crinkled = hotspots). Tent fabric handles this.

### Root Zone Mastery

**The rhizosphere is the second canopy**
- Root health = shoot health. Perfect canopy + sick roots < mediocre canopy + healthy roots.
- Root zone temp: 18–22°C optimal. Above 25°C = dissolved O₂ crash, Pythium risk. Below 15°C = uptake stalls.
- Living soil: microbial ecosystem IS the nutrient delivery. Synthetic salts defeat the purpose.

**Container choice**
- Fabric pots: air-prune (no circling), better O₂, faster dry-back. Downside: more frequent watering in flower.
- Plastic: retains moisture, cheaper. Roots circle at bottom = stagnant zone.
- Size: ScrOG 80×80 single plant = 15–20L. Multiple plants = 7–11L each.

**Irrigation principles**
- Wet/dry cycle is not optional. Roots need oxygen. "Keep moist" kills more plants than drought.
- Lift test > every moisture meter on the market.
- Runoff 10–15% even in organic — prevents salt buildup. But don't flush constantly, you're washing microbes.

### Training — Beyond Basics

**Topping vs FIM**
- Top: clean cut above node → 2 new tops. Predictable, reliable.
- FIM: pinch 80% of growth tip → 3–8 tops but chaotic. Higher reward, higher chaos.
- First grow + single-plant ScrOG: topping is the right call. FIM when comfortable.

**LST timing**
- Bend while stems are green/flexible (veg wk 3–4). Lignified stems snap.
- 90° rule: bend main to horizontal. Side branches become new mains = even canopy.
- Supercropping (intentional damage → knuckle): strengthens flow. Only on healthy, established plants.

**Defoliation — the eternal debate**
- Light defoliation (fans blocking bud sites) = universally beneficial.
- Heavy schwazzing = works at 600+ PPFD, risky under 500. At 108W in HS80 = be conservative.
- Timing: Day 1 flower (light), Day 21 (moderate). Never defoliate stressed plants.
- Rule: never >20–25% fan leaf removal at once under moderate light.

### Environment Fine-Tuning

**VPD is the master variable**
- Controls stomata → transpiration → nutrient uptake → growth rate. One number integrates temp + humidity.
- Measure at CANOPY level. Canopy VPD can differ 0.2–0.4 kPa from room.
- Leaf temp ≈ air – 2°C under LED (no IR). VPD calculators may need this offset.

**Temperature DIF**
- 5–8°C day/night difference → shorter internodes, stronger stems. Free.
- Late flower 10°C+ DIF → anthocyanin production (purples) in predisposed genetics.
- Schedule lights-on during warmest hours, lights-off during coolest.

**CO₂ reality**
- Below 600 PPFD: CO₂ supplementation is pointless. Light is the bottleneck.
- In a tent with exhaust: CO₂ bags evacuated before useful concentration. Waste of money.
- Relevant at 800+ PPFD in sealed room only. Not this setup.

### Organic & Living Soil

**Building a living soil**
- Base: quality compost or peat/coco (pH-adjusted) + 25–30% aeration (perlite, pumice, rice hulls)
- Amendments: worm castings 10–20%, kelp meal, neem meal, bone meal, rock dust (glacial/basalt)
- Inoculants: mycorrhizae (on roots at transplant), Bacillus subtilis, Trichoderma
- Philosophy: feed the soil, the soil feeds the plant.

**Compost tea (AACT)**
- Actively aerated, 24–48h brew, molasses or kelp as microbial food. Apply as soil drench.
- Science is mixed but community consensus is strongly positive for organic grows.
- NEVER foliar-spray compost tea in flower — moisture on buds = mold.

**Cover cropping in pots**
- Clover/alfalfa as living mulch: fixes N, protects surface, feeds microbes when chopped.
- Only in 15L+ pots. Smaller = competes for water.

### Unconventional Techniques — Sorted by Evidence

**Bud washing post-harvest (underrated)**
Three-bucket: 1) warm + lemon juice + baking soda, 2) warm rinse, 3) cold rinse. Removes dust, bugs, residue. Doesn't affect oil-based trichomes. Community swears by it. **Practical note:** requires space, water handling, and re-hanging wet buds — plan for this in your AR workflow.

**Gradual light reduction in late flower (interesting)**
12/12 → 11/13 → 10/14 in final 2 weeks. Mimics autumn decline, may signal accelerated ripening. Low-risk experiment.

**13/11 light cycle (promising, risky)**
Extra photon hour without revegging — some report 5–10% yield boost. Strain-dependent. Untested phenos may reveg. First grow = stick with 12/12.

**Ice water flush (debunked)** — Cold shock, P lockout, hermie risk. Skip.
**48h darkness (debunked)** — One unreplicated study, mold risk. Skip.
**Stem splitting (speculative)** — Infection risk, unproven. Not for stealth grows where plant loss = total loss.

## The "What Would a Veteran Do" Framework

When reviewing any grow plan:

1. **What's the laziest version that still works?** Simplicity reduces failure points.
2. **What breaks first?** Identify the single most likely failure and safeguard it.
3. **Unlimited budget version vs €50 version?** The gap reveals where money actually matters.
4. **Is this solving a real problem or a theoretical one?** Many "optimizations" address issues that don't exist.
5. **What does the plant want RIGHT NOW?** Not the schedule, not the forum post — what does THIS plant, THIS environment, THIS stage actually need?
