# Post-Harvest Mastery — Drying, Curing, Storage

## Core Truth

**Growing the plant is 50% of quality. Post-harvest is the other 50%.** A perfectly grown flower can be ruined by fast drying, poor curing, or bad storage. Conversely, a mediocre grow can produce surprisingly good smoke with patient, proper post-harvest handling.

---

## Harvest Decision

### Trichome Maturity (60× loupe, check CALYXES not sugar leaves)

| Stage | Appearance | Effect Profile | When to Harvest |
|---|---|---|---|
| Too early | Mostly clear/glassy | Low cannabinoid, heady/racy | Don't. |
| Peak THC | Mostly cloudy/milky, few amber | Maximum THC, cerebral, energetic | If you want uplifting/sativa-leaning effect |
| Sweet spot | 70–80% milky, 20–30% amber | Balanced potency + body. Complex terpenes at peak. | **Quality-first growers harvest here.** |
| Late | 50%+ amber | THC→CBN degradation. Heavy sedation, couch-lock. | Only for sleep aid or pain management |

**Staggered harvest (recommended):** Top colas ripen first. Cut tops, strip fan leaves from remaining lowers, give 5–7 more days under light. Lowers fatten noticeably. +10–15% total yield.

**Harvest timing within the day:** Just before lights-on. THC peaks during the dark period and partially degrades under light. Terpenes are most concentrated when cool.

## The Trim Decision

### Dry Trim (recommended for quality grows)

Leave sugar leaves intact during drying. They:
- Slow moisture loss → more even, controlled dry
- Shield trichome heads from physical damage
- Create a microclimate around buds that preserves volatile terpenes
- Are removed after drying when they're brittle and snap off cleanly

**Downside:** More labor-intensive trimming after drying. Dry trichomes are more fragile.

### Wet Trim (acceptable for efficiency)

Remove sugar leaves immediately at harvest. Advantages:
- Faster, easier trimming (leaves are turgid and easy to handle)
- Buds dry faster (sometimes too fast — the main risk)
- Cleaner look immediately

**Downside:** Faster drying = more terpene loss, harsher smoke. Exposed trichomes oxidize faster.

### The Verdict
For a quality-first grow with a single plant: **dry trim.** You have one plant — invest the time.

## Drying

### Environment — The Most Critical Phase

| Parameter | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 15–18°C | Slow evaporation preserves terpenes. Higher = hay smell. |
| Humidity | 55–65% RH | Too low (<50%) = too fast, harsh. Too high (>70%) = mold risk. |
| Airflow | Gentle, indirect | Air exchange yes. Fan pointed at buds NO. |
| Light | Complete darkness | Light degrades THC and terpenes during drying. |
| Duration | 10–14 days | Minimum 7 days. Sweet spot 10–14. If dry in <7 = too fast. |

### Using the Tent as Drying Chamber

The grow tent becomes the drying room:
**Single-tent constraint:** Drying occupies the tent for 10–14 days. Plan for a gap between cycles, or build a temporary cardboard drying chamber in the AR alongside the tent (tight but possible in 1.32m²).
1. Remove the LED (or raise to maximum height and keep off)
2. Hang branches upside-down from the ScrOG net or light hangers
3. Run the exhaust fan at LOWEST speed through the carbon filter (smell control + gentle air exchange)
4. Clip fan on lowest setting, NOT pointed at buds — aimed at wall for gentle circulation
5. Monitor temp/RH with hygrometer at canopy level

**Austrian-specific:** Winter drying is ideal — ambient 15–20°C, low humidity from central heating. Summer drying is harder — may need to run at night when cooler, or add a small fan for evaporative cooling.

### Hanging Method

**Whole plant** (recommended for single plant): Cut at base, remove large fan leaves (leave sugar leaves for dry trim), hang entire plant upside-down. Slowest, most even dry.

**Branch sections** (if whole plant won't fit): Cut branches 30–40cm, hang individually. Space so branches don't touch — touching = moisture trap = mold.

**Drying rack** (last resort): Only for small pieces or popcorn buds. Flat drying = flat side doesn't dry evenly. Rotate daily.

### How to Know It's Done

**The snap test:** Bend a small branch (~pencil thickness). When it snaps cleanly with an audible crack (not bends, not splinters), it's ready for jars. Larger branches should still bend slightly — you want the outside dry and the inside holding ~62% RH.

**If stems still bend:** Not ready. More days.
**If stems snap and buds feel crunchy/crispy:** Overdried. You can still cure but the window for peak terpene development has narrowed. Boveda packs can rehydrate slightly but can't recover lost terpenes.

## Curing

### The Science

Curing is a controlled anaerobic/microaerobic process where:
- Residual chlorophyll breaks down (removes "hay/grass" smell)
- Starches and sugars convert (smoother smoke)
- Terpene profile develops complexity (chemical reactions between terpenes create new aromatic compounds)
- THC-A continues slow decarboxylation

**This is not optional.** Uncured cannabis smells like hay, smokes harsh, and lacks the terpene complexity that defines quality flower.

### Protocol

**Jar selection:** Mason jars (Einmachgläser), wide-mouth, filled 70–80% full. Not packed tight — buds should move freely when shaken.

| Phase | Duration | Burping | What to Check |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | Days 1–7 | 2–3× daily, 10–15 min each | Ammonia smell = too wet, leave lids off 1hr. Moisture rebalancing. |
| **Week 2** | Days 8–14 | 1× daily, 5–10 min | Smell transitioning from hay → strain-specific aroma. Moisture evening out. |
| **Week 3** | Days 15–21 | Every 2–3 days, 5 min | Aroma developing. Smoke test possible but patience pays. |
| **Week 4+** | Days 22+ | Weekly, or sealed with Boveda 62% | Minimum cure complete. Flavor and smoothness continue improving for months. |

### Troubleshooting Cure Issues

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Ammonia smell on opening** | Too wet. Anaerobic bacteria active. | Leave lids off 2–4 hours. Check all buds for mold. Re-jar. Burp more frequently. |
| **Hay/grass smell persists past week 2** | Chlorophyll not breaking down, likely dried too fast | Patience. May take 3–4 weeks. Add Boveda 62% to maintain humidity. Can't fully fix fast-dry. |
| **Mold in jar** | Jarred too wet, or contamination from drying | Remove ALL affected buds + any touching them. Check remaining with loupe. Re-dry remaining before re-jarring. |
| **Buds feel too dry / won't rehydrate** | Overdried before jarring | Boveda 62% pack brings back some moisture but lost terpenes don't return. Still smoke fine, just less aromatic. |
| **Harsh smoke after 4+ weeks** | Either too much unflushed nutrient (less likely with organics) or insufficient cure time | Keep curing. Organic grows rarely have harshness issues. Try 6–8 weeks. |

### Humidity Management in Jars

**Target: 58–65% RH inside the jar**

- Mini hygrometer in jar (€2–3 each) = the real-time gauge. Worth every cent.
- Boveda 62% packs: two-way humidity control. Add from week 2 onward. They absorb excess moisture AND release if too dry.
- If RH reads >70%: leave lid off, re-check in 1–2 hours. If >75%: buds need more drying time outside jars.
- If RH reads <55%: Boveda will help but the cure will be slower. Acceptable, just less optimal.

## Long-Term Storage (>2 months)

| Factor | Requirement | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Light | Complete darkness | THC degrades under light (UV especially). Mason jars are not light-proof — store in dark cupboard or wrap in cloth. |
| Temperature | 15–20°C | Higher = faster degradation. **Fridge: NOT recommended** — condensation forms every time jar is opened in warm air, creating mold risk. Room temp in a dark, cool spot is safer. |
| Humidity | 58–62% RH (Boveda) | Below 55% = trichomes become brittle and fall off. Above 65% = mold risk. |
| Oxygen | Minimal headspace | Fill jars 70–80%. Less air = less oxidation. Vacuum seal for 3+ month storage. |
| Container | Glass (mason jar), not plastic | Plastic generates static that pulls trichomes off bud surface. Glass is inert. |

**Degradation timeline:**
- 0–6 months: Peak quality window if stored properly.
- 6–12 months: Slight terpene degradation but still excellent. THC barely affected.
- 12–24 months: Noticeable terpene loss. THC→CBN conversion begins (~5–10% per year at room temp in dark).
- 24+ months: Still smokeable but quality is clearly diminished.

## Trim Waste — Don't Throw It Away

**Sugar leaf trim:** Contains significant trichomes. Options:
- **Dry ice hash:** Simplest method. Dry ice + trim in bubble bag, shake over glass. 30–50% return of blonde trichome heads.
- **Bubble hash (ice water):** More equipment but cleaner product. Multiple bag sizes for quality separation.
- **Butter/oil infusion:** Decarb trim (110°C, 40 min), infuse into butter or coconut oil. Simple, effective.
- **Rosin press (if you invest):** Heat + pressure = solventless extract. Direct from trim or from bubble hash.

**Fan leaves:** Minimal trichomes but usable for edible infusions or composting back into living soil.

**Stems:** Save larger stems — they can be used for cannabutter (low potency but not zero) or discarded. In a stealth grow, disposal matters: shred/break down stems and mix with regular kitchen waste. Don't throw recognizable stems in common building trash.

## Post-Harvest Stealth Notes

- Drying and fresh-trimmed cannabis produces MORE smell than growing. Run AKF 24/7 during drying.
- Burp jars inside the AR with door closed. NEVER in living areas.
- Cure jars stored in AR or inside a sealed container (Tupperware) as secondary smell barrier.
- Trim waste disposal: seal in bags, take directly to outdoor waste. Don't accumulate in apartment.
- Cleaning post-harvest: wipe down tent, scissors, surfaces. Resin residue has persistent odor.
